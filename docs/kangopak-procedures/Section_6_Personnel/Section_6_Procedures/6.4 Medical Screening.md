# Procedure 6.4: Medical Screening Procedure

**Company:** Kangopak
**Document Type:** Operational Procedure
**Revision:** 1
**Revised Date:** 2025-09-24
**Document Owner:** Human Resources Manager
**Status:** Current

---

## Purpose

To establish comprehensive medical screening procedures that protect product safety through appropriate health awareness, symptom notification systems, and exclusion protocols for personnel, visitors, and contractors, in full compliance with requirements for medical screening (Clause 6.4).

This procedure ensures that individuals with symptoms, infections, diseases, or conditions that could compromise product safety are appropriately managed and excluded from product contact activities.

## Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All Kangopak personnel (permanent, temporary, contract)
- All visitors accessing manufacturing, packing, or storage areas
- All contractors working in production-related areas
- Personnel in direct contact with product or food contact materials
- Personnel in hygiene-sensitive product handling areas

## Responsibilities

### Human Resources Manager

- Overall responsibility for medical screening program
- Development and maintenance of health questionnaires
- Management of medical exclusion decisions and records
- Liaison with healthcare professionals and legal advisors
- Training program coordination for health awareness

### Operations Manager

- Implementation of screening procedures in operational areas
- Coordination of visitor and contractor screening
- Management of work exclusion and return-to-work processes
- Integration of medical screening with operational procedures

### Department Supervisors

- Daily monitoring of personnel health status
- Implementation of symptom notification procedures
- Coordination of temporary work reassignments
- Communication with HR regarding health concerns

### All Personnel

- Completion of health awareness training
- Notification of relevant health symptoms or conditions
- Compliance with medical exclusion requirements
- Maintenance of personal health records as required

## Requirements Implementation

### 6.4.1 Personnel Health Awareness and Notification

**Official Requirement**: *"The site shall make personnel aware of the symptoms of infection, disease or condition which would prevent a person working in contact with the product. It shall be based on risk to the intended use of the finished product, or where local legislation is in place. The site shall have a procedure which enables notification by personnel (including temporary personnel) of any relevant symptoms, infection, disease or condition, which they may have been in contact with or may be suffering from. Where there may be a risk to product safety, personnel suffering from any of the above shall be excluded from work involving contact with the product the direct handling of food contact or hygiene-sensitive product for as long as the symptoms persist."*

#### Health Awareness Program

**A. Risk-Based Health Awareness**
Based on packaging materials for food contact, personnel awareness focuses on:

**Gastrointestinal Conditions**:

- **Diarrhea and vomiting**: Symptoms that indicate gastrointestinal illness
- **Stomach cramps and nausea**: Early symptoms of gastrointestinal distress
- **Foodborne illness exposure**: Contact with individuals with food poisoning
- **Infectious gastroenteritis**: Viral, bacterial, or parasitic gastrointestinal infections

**Respiratory Conditions**:

- **Persistent coughing**: Coughing that could result in respiratory droplet contamination
- **Flu-like symptoms**: Fever, body aches, respiratory symptoms
- **COVID-19 symptoms**: Current pandemic-related symptom awareness
- **Tuberculosis exposure**: Contact with individuals with tuberculosis

**Skin and Wound Conditions**:

- **Open wounds and cuts**: Particularly on hands and arms exposed to product contact
- **Infected wounds**: Wounds showing signs of infection or delayed healing
- **Skin infections**: Bacterial, viral, or fungal skin infections
- **Rashes and dermatitis**: Skin conditions that could transfer contaminants

**Other Relevant Conditions**:

- **Hepatitis A and E**: Liver infections transmissible through contamination
- **Jaundice**: Yellow coloration of skin or eyes indicating liver condition
- **Conjunctivitis**: Eye infections that could transfer to hands and products
- **Fever of unknown origin**: Elevated body temperature without clear cause

#### Health Awareness Training

**A. Training Content**
All personnel receive training covering:

- **Symptom recognition**: How to identify relevant health symptoms
- **Risk understanding**: Why these conditions pose risks to product safety
- **Notification requirements**: When and how to report health concerns
- **Exclusion procedures**: What happens when exclusion is necessary
- **Return-to-work requirements**: Conditions for returning to work after illness

**B. Training Delivery**

- **Initial training**: Provided during employee orientation
- **Annual refresher**: Annual update training for all personnel
- **Seasonal updates**: Updates for seasonal illness awareness (flu season, etc.)
- **Incident-based training**: Additional training following health incidents

**C. Training Documentation**

- **Training records**: Documentation of training completion for all personnel
- **Competency assessment**: Assessment of understanding of health requirements
- **Training materials**: Current training materials and resources
- **Update tracking**: Tracking of training updates and changes

#### Symptom Notification System

**A. Notification Process**
Personnel must notify their supervisor and HR immediately when experiencing:

- Any symptoms listed in health awareness training
- Contact with individuals with infectious conditions
- Diagnosis of any communicable disease
- Any condition that could compromise product safety

**B. Notification Methods**

- **Direct supervisor notification**: Immediate notification to direct supervisor
- **HR notification**: Notification to HR Manager within 24 hours
- **Anonymous reporting**: Anonymous reporting system for sensitive health issues
- **Emergency notification**: After-hours notification procedures for urgent situations

**C. Notification Documentation**

- **Symptom notification forms**: Standardized forms for symptom reporting
- **Confidential records**: Confidential maintenance of health records
- **Follow-up tracking**: Tracking of symptom progression and resolution
- **Return-to-work documentation**: Documentation of clearance to return to work

#### Work Exclusion Management

**A. Exclusion Criteria**
Personnel are excluded from product contact work when suffering from:

- **Active gastrointestinal symptoms**: Diarrhea, vomiting, stomach cramps
- **Respiratory symptoms with fever**: Fever with coughing, sneezing, or sore throat
- **Open infected wounds**: Wounds on hands, arms, or other exposed areas
- **Diagnosed communicable diseases**: Diseases transmissible through contamination
- **Other risk conditions**: Any condition deemed a risk to product safety

**B. Exclusion Decision Process**

- **Initial assessment**: Immediate assessment by supervisor and HR
- **Risk evaluation**: Evaluation of risk to product safety
- **Medical consultation**: Consultation with healthcare professional if needed
- **Exclusion decision**: Formal decision on work exclusion
- **Documentation**: Documentation of exclusion decision and rationale

**C. Alternative Work Arrangements**
During exclusion, options may include:

- **Non-product contact work**: Administrative or maintenance duties away from production
- **Remote work**: Work-from-home arrangements where applicable
- **Sick leave**: Use of sick leave benefits during illness
- **Unpaid leave**: Unpaid leave if other options are not available

**D. Return-to-Work Requirements**
Personnel may return to product contact work when:

- **Symptom-free period**: Free from symptoms for specified period (typically 48-72 hours)
- **Medical clearance**: Medical clearance from healthcare provider if required
- **Negative test results**: Negative test results for specific conditions if required
- **Management approval**: Approval from supervisor and HR to return to work

### 6.4.2 Visitor and Contractor Health Screening

**Official Requirement**: *"Where permitted by law, visitors and contractors shall be required to complete a health questionnaire or otherwise confirm that they are not suffering from any symptoms of infection, disease or conditions which may put product safety at risk, prior to being allowed into manufacturing, packing or storage areas."*

#### Legal Compliance Assessment

**A. South African Legal Requirements**

- **Privacy legislation**: Compliance with Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA)
- **Employment legislation**: Compliance with employment and labor laws
- **Healthcare legislation**: Compliance with healthcare privacy and access requirements
- **Discrimination prevention**: Ensuring health screening does not discriminate unfairly

**B. Permitted Health Screening**
Within legal framework, health screening includes:

- **General health questionnaire**: General questions about current health status
- **Symptom declaration**: Declaration of current symptoms relevant to product safety
- **Contact exposure declaration**: Declaration of exposure to communicable diseases
- **Consent to screening**: Consent to health screening for facility access

#### Health Questionnaire for Visitors and Contractors

**A. Pre-Entry Health Questions**
Visitors and contractors must confirm they are not currently experiencing:

- **Gastrointestinal symptoms**: Diarrhea, vomiting, nausea, stomach cramps
- **Respiratory symptoms**: Persistent cough, fever, flu-like symptoms
- **Skin conditions**: Open wounds, infected cuts, skin infections on exposed areas
- **Other infectious conditions**: Any diagnosed infectious or communicable disease

**B. Contact Exposure Questions**
Visitors and contractors must declare if they have had recent contact with:

- **Individuals with foodborne illness**: Contact with people with food poisoning
- **Individuals with respiratory illness**: Contact with people with flu, COVID-19, etc.
- **Individuals with communicable diseases**: Contact with people with infectious diseases
- **Healthcare settings**: Recent visits to hospitals or healthcare facilities for infectious disease treatment

**C. Travel and Environmental Exposure**
Where relevant, visitors and contractors may be asked about:

- **Recent travel**: Travel to areas with disease outbreaks
- **Environmental exposure**: Exposure to contaminated environments
- **Occupational exposure**: Work in environments with infectious disease risk

#### Screening Implementation

**A. Screening Process**

1. **Pre-arrival notification**: Advance notification of health screening requirements
2. **Arrival screening**: Health questionnaire completion upon arrival
3. **Visual assessment**: Visual assessment for obvious signs of illness
4. **Decision and documentation**: Decision on facility access and documentation
5. **Follow-up**: Follow-up if health concerns are identified

**B. Access Decision Process**

- **Questionnaire review**: Review of completed health questionnaire
- **Risk assessment**: Assessment of risk to product safety
- **Alternative arrangements**: Consideration of alternative meeting/work arrangements
- **Access decision**: Decision on facility access
- **Documentation**: Documentation of screening results and decisions

**C. Alternative Arrangements**
For visitors/contractors with health concerns:

- **Remote meetings**: Video conferencing or phone meetings instead of in-person visits
- **Delayed visits**: Postponing visits until health concerns are resolved
- **Limited access**: Access to non-production areas only
- **Enhanced precautions**: Enhanced protective measures for essential visits
