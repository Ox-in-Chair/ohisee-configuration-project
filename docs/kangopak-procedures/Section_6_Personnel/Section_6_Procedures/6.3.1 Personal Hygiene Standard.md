# Procedure 6.3.1: BRC Personal Hygiene Standard

**Company:** Kangopak
**Document Type:** Operational Procedure
**Revision:** 17
**Revised Date:** 2025-09-02
**Document Owner:** Operations Manager
**Status:** Current

---

## HEALTH & HYGIENE

### 1. Hand Washing and Sanitisation

On entering the Factory, all personnel must adhere to the designated walkways and proceed to the washing facility, where they must wash their hands properly using hot water and liquid soap, before entering the manufacturing area the employees must sanitise their hands using the sanitisers provided.

**Hands are to be washed and sanitised:**

- At the beginning of a shift
- After lunch or tea breaks
- After going to the toilet
- After smoking
- When hands are dirty during operation
- Immediately after handling any potentially contaminated material

Washrooms to be kept tidy.

### 2. Health Monitoring and Reporting

Kangopak makes all personnel, including temporary staff and visitors, aware of the symptoms of infection, disease, or conditions that could compromise product safety, such as vomiting, diarrhoea, jaundice, fever, sore throat, nasal drip, and open sores. These symptoms are communicated during induction and refresher training and displayed on the Personal Hygiene Standard posters throughout the site.

Personnel must notify their Team Leader immediately if they experience any listed symptoms or have been in contact with infectious diseases. Any affected individuals are excluded from handling food-contact or hygiene-sensitive products until they are symptom-free or medically cleared. This process is supported by the completion of Medical Screening Questionnaires (6.3F3) for all new hires and routine supervisor checks.

**Personnel who have any of the following complaints must report to the Team Leader:**

- Coughing and sneezing
- Jaundice
- Vomiting
- Fever and sore throat and fever
- Nasal drip
- Hay fever and Allergies
- Diarrhoea
- Open sores / Injuries on hands and forearms (boils, cuts or sores)
- Discharges from the ear, eye or nose

### 3. Injury Management

Personnel who are injured on duty (minor or serious) must report to the Team Leader immediately.

- The person must receive First Aid treatment
- All wounds and burns on exposed skin shall be covered by a coloured plaster. These shall be issued and monitored by a First Responder using Plaster Register 6.3F2. Where appropriate, in addition to the plaster, a finger stall or glove shall be worn
- If the person cannot be removed from the scene, an ambulance must be summonsed. The Team Leader will phone an ambulance, while First Aid is rendered to the injured person
- Necessary documentation must be completed. (Accident report form)
- The Operations Manager and Team Leader will do the investigation of the injury

Completed documents for injuries on nightshift, must be forwarded to the Operations Manager on the first working day after the incident.

## PROTECTIVE CLOTHING

### 1. PPE Requirements

Protective clothing is to be worn by all persons entering the production and storage which are high - medium risk areas.

- Men must wear clean two-piece suits
- Ladies must wear clean "Ladies" suits or dustcoats
- Management, general non-process staff, and visitors wear clean dustcoats
- On leaving their work station to go to the toilet, eat, or leave the premises all employees must remove the top half of their PPE and hang same on hooks at the base of the change room stairs
- On leaving their work station to go to the designated smoking area all employees must remove the top half of their PPE and hang same on hooks at the base of the change room stairs. Supplied green dust coats to be worn

Contractors to supply their own protective clothing.

### 2. PPE Standards

Where worn, sufficient, site-issued sets of protective clothing shall be provided.

Protective clothing worn by personnel, temporary personnel, contractors and visitors in manufacturing and storage areas shall be clean and provide adequate coverage of personal clothing.

The protective clothing shall have no external pockets on the upper body garments or sewn-on buttons.

Drivers of Forklift and Company Vehicles to wear reflective vests.

Storage of clean protective clothing on site shall be controlled to prevent cross-contamination.

### 3. Hair and Head Protection

All personnel and visitors entering the production and storage areas must wear hairnets at all times. Hairnets must cover all hair.

In production and storage areas beards and moustaches must be protected by restraints.

### 4. Footwear

Safety shoes must be worn, by all process and packing personnel.

Personnel and visitors must wear closed shoes at all times.

### 5. PPE Maintenance

Personal Protective equipment must be designed to prevent product contamination and maintained in hygienic condition.

Changes of protective clothing shall be available at all times as required.

Damaged clothing must be replaced immediately.

### 6. General Safety Requirements

These standards apply to all personnel, permanent and temporary, as well as visitors and suppliers.

Ear Protection must be worn by all personnel and visitors when entering designated noise areas.

**Employees must report abnormal incidents that may occur:**

- Near misses
- Accidents
- IOD's
- Occurrences out of the ordinary
- Foreign objects
- Glass/Plastic breakage
- Breakage/Loss of blades

## BEHAVIOUR

### 1. Jewellery & Perfume/Aftershave

No jewellery and wrist bands, wrist-worn devices or watches shall not be worn are allowed to be worn on exposed parts of the body in the production or storage areas, with the exception of a plain wedding ring.

All personal items and belongings including personal mobile telephones must not be taken into production areas without the permission of the management.

Medical bracelets are allowed, but must be covered with a bracelet net.

Visitors have access to the storage facility for locking of valuables which is available at the Reception desk.

No perfume / aftershave to be worn in the factory.

No Writing implements behind the ears are allowed.

### 2. Eating and Drinking

No eating or drinking is permitted in the factory. (which includes production, change rooms, toilets, workshop and warehouse).

NO FOOD i.e allergens – cereals, eggs, cow's milk, soy, fish, crustaceans & molluscs, peanuts and tree nuts are permitted in the factory (which includes production, change rooms, toilets, workshop and warehouse).

No eating includes the chewing of gum, straws, matches, tobacco etc.

Drinking of water from purpose-made dispensers and /or by using disposable conical cups or spill-proof container is allowed in the designated identified areas.

No bottles, cans or cups will be allowed in the areas described above.

No food or drink should be stored in the lockers from shift to shift.

Personnel shall be required to refrain from sneezing or coughing over materials or products. Spitting (expectorating) is prohibited.

### 3. Smoking

Smoking (including the use of electronic cigarettes) is not allowed in the production area. It is only allowed in designated areas outside all buildings. Cigarette butts must be placed in buckets provided.

### 4. Incident Reporting

Personnel need to report any risks or any evidence of unsafe or out-of- specification product, equipment or raw materials, to the Team Leader to enable the resolution of issues requiring immediate action.

### 5. Medicines

Based on the risk assessment above. Chronic medication like Asthma Pump to be placed in allocated compartment in First Aid Box. No other medication be taken into the production facility. It may be kept in personal lockers.

### 6. Lockers

Personal lockers must be maintained so that they are kept free from rubbish and soiled clothing.

No product contact tools, food and equipment are allowed to be kept in personal lockers.

### 7. Gloves

If gloves are used, they must be replaced regularly. Where appropriate, gloves are of a disposable type, of a distinctive colour, intact and not shed loose fibres.
