# Contractor and Visitor Control Training

**Module:** Contractor and Visitor Control  
**Audience:** All factory personnel, particularly supervisors and reception staff  
**BRCGS Reference:** 4.4 Security, 6.1.1 Site Standards  
**Kangopak Procedure:** 4.6 BRCGS Security

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

1. **Identify** requirements for contractor and visitor access control
2. **Implement** proper supervision and monitoring of contractors and visitors
3. **Ensure** compliance with site standards and security procedures
4. **Report** any security concerns or non-compliance issues

## Why Contractor and Visitor Control Matters

Contractors and visitors can introduce security risks, contamination hazards, or safety issues if not properly controlled. Proper management protects products, facilities, and personnel.

**Key BRCGS Requirement (4.4):** Site security must include appropriate controls for contractors and visitors to prevent unauthorised access and maintain product safety.

**Kangopak Standard:** All contractors and visitors must be supervised, complete visitor registration, and comply with site standards throughout their visit.

## Types of People Requiring Control

### Contractors
- **Maintenance technicians:** External specialists for equipment repair
- **Construction workers:** Building and facility improvement contractors
- **Service providers:** Cleaning, pest control, and utility contractors
- **Delivery drivers:** Transport and logistics personnel
- **Installation specialists:** Equipment installation and commissioning contractors

### Visitors
- **Customer representatives:** Buyers, quality auditors, and technical specialists
- **Regulatory inspectors:** Government officials and certification auditors
- **Suppliers:** Raw material and equipment suppliers
- **Professional advisors:** Consultants, lawyers, and technical experts
- **Job candidates:** People attending interviews or site visits

### Emergency Personnel
- **Emergency services:** Police, fire brigade, and ambulance crews
- **Utility workers:** Gas, electricity, and water emergency responders
- **Government officials:** Environmental health and safety inspectors
- **Insurance assessors:** Personnel investigating incidents or claims

## Access Control Procedures

### Pre-Arrival Requirements
- **Advance notification:** All visits must be scheduled and approved in advance
- **Purpose identification:** Clear reason for visit must be documented
- **Escort assignment:** Designated Kangopak employee assigned as escort
- **Security clearance:** Background checks for regular contractors
- **Site briefing:** Information about site rules and safety requirements provided

### Arrival Procedures
1. **Reception check-in:** All visitors and contractors report to main reception
2. **Identity verification:** Photo ID checked and recorded
3. **Visitor register completion:** Mandatory registration with full details
4. **Site standards briefing:** Safety and security requirements explained
5. **PPE issue:** Appropriate protective equipment provided if required
6. **Escort assignment:** Designated supervisor or escort assigned

### During Visit Procedures
- **Continuous supervision:** Designated Kangopak employee monitors activities
- **Area restrictions:** Visitors limited to authorised areas only
- **Safety compliance:** All site safety rules must be followed
- **Security awareness:** Escort responsible for preventing unauthorised access
- **Documentation:** Activities and areas visited recorded as required

## Visitor Registration System

### Required Information
- **Personal details:** Full name, company, and contact information
- **Visit purpose:** Specific reason for visit and expected duration
- **Escort details:** Name and department of assigned Kangopak employee
- **Areas to visit:** Specific locations visitor is authorised to access
- **Date and time:** Arrival and expected departure times

### Registration Process
1. **Complete visitor register:** All required fields must be filled in
2. **Escort confirmation:** Designated employee confirms availability and responsibility
3. **Authority approval:** Supervisor approval for access to restricted areas
4. **Badge issuance:** Temporary visitor badge provided for identification
5. **Safety briefing:** Site-specific safety and security information provided

### Special Requirements
- **Photo identification:** Valid photo ID must be presented and verified
- **Company credentials:** Business cards or company identification preferred
- **Emergency contacts:** Contact information for visitor's organisation
- **Medical conditions:** Any relevant health or medical issues disclosed
- **Language requirements:** Translation services arranged if needed

## Supervision Requirements

### Designated Kangopak Supervisor
- **Primary contact:** Specific employee responsible for contractor/visitor activities
- **Continuous monitoring:** Present throughout visit or work period
- **Authority and accountability:** Responsible for ensuring compliance with all site rules
- **Communication liaison:** Interface between visitor/contractor and site management
- **Safety responsibility:** Ensure visitor/contractor safety throughout visit

### Supervision Standards
- **Physical presence:** Supervisor must be physically present during activities
- **Knowledge requirements:** Supervisor must understand relevant safety and security procedures
- **Authority level:** Supervisor must have authority to stop work if safety concerns arise
- **Documentation duties:** Record activities and any issues that arise
- **Communication skills:** Able to effectively communicate site requirements

### Maintenance Technician Role
- **Contractor coordination:** Maintenance Technician is primary contact for maintenance contractors
- **Technical oversight:** Provides technical supervision and guidance
- **Safety monitoring:** Ensures maintenance work follows safety procedures
- **Quality assurance:** Verifies work meets Kangopak standards
- **Documentation:** Records maintenance activities and contractor performance

## Site Standards Compliance

### Safety Requirements
- **Personal protective equipment:** Appropriate PPE must be worn at all times
- **Safety briefing:** Comprehensive briefing on site-specific hazards and controls
- **Emergency procedures:** Information about evacuation routes and emergency contacts
- **Restricted areas:** Clear identification of areas that are off-limits
- **Equipment safety:** Safe use of tools and equipment

### Security Requirements
- **Badge wearing:** Visitor badges must be visible at all times
- **Escort accompaniment:** Visitors must remain with designated escort
- **Photography restrictions:** No unauthorised photography or recording
- **Information confidentiality:** Respect for confidential business information
- **Access limitations:** Stay within authorised areas only

### Hygiene Requirements
- **Hand washing:** Proper hand hygiene before entering food packaging areas
- **Protective clothing:** Clean protective clothing provided and worn correctly
- **Personal items:** Restrictions on personal belongings in production areas
- **Food and drink:** Only in designated areas, not in production zones
- **Health screening:** Disclosure of any illness that might affect Product Safety

## Practical Factory Examples

### DO's ✓
- **Check credentials:** Always verify identity and purpose of visit
- **Complete registration:** Ensure all visitor registration details are recorded
- **Provide escort:** Assign competent supervisor for duration of visit
- **Monitor continuously:** Keep visitors/contractors under appropriate supervision
- **Document activities:** Record what was done and any issues encountered

### DON'Ts ✗
- **Never allow unescorted access:** All visitors/contractors must be supervised
- **Never skip registration:** All visits must be properly documented
- **Never ignore safety:** Always ensure safety requirements are met
- **Never compromise security:** Follow all security procedures without exception
- **Never assume competence:** Verify that visitors/contractors understand site requirements

## Emergency Situations

### Unidentified Persons on Site
1. **Approach safely:** Challenge person politely but firmly
2. **Request identification:** Ask for photo ID and purpose of visit
3. **Escort to reception:** Accompany to reception for proper registration
4. **Report immediately:** Inform security or management of unauthorised person
5. **Follow up:** Ensure proper procedures followed and documented

### Security Incidents
- **Immediate response:** Address immediate safety concerns
- **Report to management:** Notify appropriate managers immediately
- **Document incident:** Complete incident report with full details
- **Investigation cooperation:** Assist with any investigation required
- **Corrective action:** Implement measures to prevent similar incidents

### Evacuation Procedures
- **Include visitors:** Ensure all visitors/contractors included in evacuation
- **Account for personnel:** Check that all visitors reached assembly point
- **Provide assistance:** Help visitors who may be unfamiliar with evacuation routes
- **Report to emergency services:** Inform emergency responders about visitors on site
- **Post-emergency procedures:** Ensure visitors are safely managed after emergency

## Special Situations

### Out-of-Hours Visits
- **Advance authorisation:** Management approval required for after-hours visits
- **Enhanced security:** Additional security measures may be required
- **Senior supervision:** Senior staff member must supervise out-of-hours activities
- **Emergency contacts:** Ensure emergency contact information available
- **Documentation:** Extra care with documentation for out-of-hours visits

### Long-Term Contractors
- **Extended supervision:** Ongoing monitoring for contractors working multiple days
- **Competency verification:** Regular assessment of contractor performance
- **Relationship management:** Build working relationship while maintaining control
- **Progress monitoring:** Track progress against agreed timelines and standards
- **Final sign-off:** Formal completion and sign-off procedures

## Record Keeping and Documentation

### Visitor Register
- **Daily records:** All visits recorded with complete information
- **Retention period:** Visitor registers kept for required period
- **Access control:** Registers stored securely to protect personal information
- **Audit trail:** Records available for internal and external audits
- **Data protection:** Comply with data protection regulations

### Incident Reporting
- **Security incidents:** Any security-related issues documented
- **Safety incidents:** Accidents or near-misses involving visitors/contractors
- **Non-compliance:** Failures to follow site standards or procedures
- **Corrective actions:** Actions taken to address incidents and prevent recurrence
- **Management review:** Regular review of incident patterns and trends

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **What must all contractors and visitors do when arriving at Kangopak?**
   - Answer: Report to reception, complete visitor register, receive safety briefing, and be assigned a designated supervisor

2. **Who is primarily responsible for supervising maintenance contractors?**
   - Answer: The Maintenance Technician serves as primary contact and supervisor for maintenance contractors

3. **What should you do if you see an unidentified person in the factory?**
   - Answer: Approach safely, ask for identification, escort to reception for registration, and report to management

4. **What information must be recorded in the visitor register?**
   - Answer: Personal details, visit purpose, escort details, areas to visit, and date/time information
