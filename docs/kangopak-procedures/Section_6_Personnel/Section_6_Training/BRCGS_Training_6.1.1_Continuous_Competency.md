# Continuous Competency Development Training

**Module:** Continuous Competency Development  
**Audience:** All personnel  
**BRCGS Reference:** 6.1.6  
**Kangopak Procedure:** 6.1.1 BRC Training and Competence Procedure

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

1. **Understand** what competency means in your role and why continuous development is essential
2. **Participate** actively in competency assessment and development planning processes
3. **Take responsibility** for your ongoing learning and skill development at Kangopak

## Why Continuous Competency Matters

Continuous competency ensures you have the knowledge, skills, and attitudes needed to perform your job safely and effectively. As our business evolves, customer requirements change, and new technologies emerge, everyone needs to keep learning and developing.

**Key BRCGS Requirement (6.1.6):** The site shall routinely review and document the competencies of all personnel and provide relevant training as appropriate.

**Kangopak Commitment:** Staff training is reviewed at minimum during annual Management Review, with ongoing competency development through various learning opportunities and performance monitoring.

## Understanding Competency

### What Is Competency?
Competency is the combination of:
- **Knowledge:** What you need to know to do your job
- **Skills:** What you need to be able to do practically
- **Attitude:** How you approach your work and responsibilities
- **Application:** Using knowledge and skills effectively in real situations

### Components of Job Competency
**Technical competency:**
- **Product knowledge** relevant to your role
- **Process understanding** for your work area
- **Equipment operation** skills for your tasks
- **Quality requirements** for your outputs
- **Safety procedures** for your activities

**Behavioural competency:**
- **Communication skills** for your interactions
- **Problem-solving ability** for your challenges
- **Teamwork capabilities** for your environment
- **Leadership qualities** appropriate to your level
- **Adaptability** for changing requirements

### Why Competency Development Never Stops
**Business changes:**
- **New customers** with different requirements
- **Equipment upgrades** requiring new skills
- **Process improvements** needing updated knowledge
- **Regulatory changes** affecting procedures
- **Technology advances** creating opportunities

**Personal development:**
- **Career advancement** requires broader competencies
- **Job enrichment** through additional responsibilities
- **Knowledge refresh** to maintain current skills
- **Skill enhancement** for improved performance
- **Confidence building** through mastery

## Competency Assessment Process

### Regular Review Schedule
**Annual assessment:**
- **Formal review** of all competencies required for your role
- **Performance evaluation** against established standards
- **Development planning** for the coming year
- **Training needs identification** based on gaps or changes
- **Career discussion** about your aspirations and opportunities

**Ongoing monitoring:**
- **Supervisor observation** of your daily performance
- **Peer feedback** on your collaborative effectiveness
- **Self-assessment** of your confidence and capability
- **Customer feedback** where relevant to your role
- **Incident analysis** to identify learning needs

### Assessment Methods
**Observation:**
- **Direct observation** of your work practices
- **Skill demonstration** for practical competencies
- **Problem-solving** assessment during real situations
- **Communication evaluation** during interactions
- **Leadership observation** for supervisory roles

**Testing and evaluation:**
- **Knowledge tests** for technical understanding
- **Practical assessments** for skill demonstration
- **Case study exercises** for decision-making ability
- **Presentation tasks** for communication skills
- **Project work** for comprehensive competency demonstration

### Documentation
**Competency records include:**
- **Current competency status** for all required areas
- **Evidence** of competency demonstration
- **Development plans** with specific objectives and timelines
- **Training completed** and effectiveness evaluation
- **Performance trends** over time

## Individual Development Planning

### Self-Assessment
**Honest evaluation of:**
- **Current strengths** in your role
- **Areas for improvement** you've identified
- **Knowledge gaps** affecting your performance
- **Skills you'd like to develop** for career progression
- **Challenges** you find difficult in your work

### Goal Setting
**SMART objectives:**
- **Specific:** Clear description of what you want to achieve
- **Measurable:** How you'll know you've succeeded
- **Achievable:** Realistic given your situation and resources
- **Relevant:** Important for your role and career
- **Time-bound:** Clear deadline for achievement

### Action Planning
**Development activities:**
- **Training courses** to address knowledge gaps
- **Practice opportunities** to develop skills
- **Mentoring relationships** for guidance and support
- **Project work** to apply new learning
- **Regular review** to track progress and adjust plans

## Practical Factory Examples

### Effective Competency Development ✓
- **Proactive learning:** "I noticed I struggled with the new equipment, so I asked for additional training and practiced during quiet periods"
- **Peer support:** "I helped train the new team member on quality procedures because I'd recently been through the updated training"
- **Application focus:** "After the customer requirements training, I started checking specifications more carefully and caught two potential issues"
- **Feedback seeking:** "I asked my supervisor for specific feedback on my problem-solving approach so I could improve"

### Missed Development Opportunities ✗
- **Passive approach:** "I just attend training when I'm told to - I don't think about what else I need to learn"
- **Application failure:** "I went on the course but I don't really use what I learned in my daily work"
- **Isolation:** "I prefer to figure things out myself rather than ask for help or coaching"
- **Status quo:** "I've been doing this job for years the same way - why do I need to change?"

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **What are the three main components that make up job competency?**
   - Answer: Knowledge (what you need to know), Skills (what you need to do), Attitude (how you approach work), and Application (using them effectively)

2. **Name three different ways you can develop your competency at work.**
   - Answer: Formal training courses, on-the-job coaching/mentoring, job rotation, project assignments, self-directed learning (any three)

3. **Why is continuous competency development important even if you're experienced in your current role?**
   - Answer: Business changes require new skills, regulations update, technology advances, career development needs broader competencies, maintains confidence and motivation

## Simple Practical Activity (12 minutes)

**Personal Development Planning:**

1. **Self-assessment:** Rate your current competency in 5 key areas of your job (1-5 scale)
2. **Gap identification:** Identify your biggest development need
3. **Learning preference:** What type of development activity would work best for you?
4. **Goal setting:** Write one SMART development objective
5. **Action planning:** List 3 specific actions you could take to achieve your objective
6. **Support identification:** Who could help you with your development?
7. **Review planning:** When will you review progress on your development goal?

**Sharing:** Volunteers share their development goals with the group for feedback and support.

## What Supervisors Look For

Your supervisor will assess:

- **Self-awareness** - realistic understanding of your strengths and development needs
- **Learning attitude** - enthusiasm for developing new knowledge and skills
- **Application effectiveness** - using new learning to improve job performance
- **Development ownership** - taking responsibility for your own competency development
- **Feedback receptiveness** - openness to constructive feedback and coaching
- **Continuous improvement** - ongoing commitment to learning and development

**Regular review:** Competency and development are reviewed routinely with documentation of progress and plans.
