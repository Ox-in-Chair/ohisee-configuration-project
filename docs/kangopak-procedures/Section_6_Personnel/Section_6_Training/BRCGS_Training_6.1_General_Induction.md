## Company Hygiene Policies

### Personal Hygiene Requirements
**Before starting work each day:**
- **Hand washing** thoroughly with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds
- **Clean clothing** - put on fresh uniform provided by Kangopak
- **Hair covering** completely covered with hat or net
- **Personal items** stored in lockers, not brought into production areas
- **Health check** - don't work if you have symptoms that could affect Product Safety

**During work:**
- **Maintain cleanliness** throughout your shift
- **Wash hands** after breaks, toilet visits, or changing activities
- **Keep work area tidy** to prevent contamination sources
- **Report problems** immediately if hygiene standards can't be maintained

### What's Not Allowed
**Items prohibited in production areas:**
- **Jewellery** except plain wedding rings
- **Food and drink** - only in designated break areas
- **Mobile phones** - keep in lockers
- **Strong perfumes** or cosmetics that could contaminate products
- **Personal medications** unless approved and properly stored

**Behaviours not permitted:**
- **Eating, chewing, or smoking** in production areas
- **Touching face or hair** while handling packaging materials
- **Working when ill** with symptoms that could contaminate products
- **Ignoring hygiene procedures** even when in a hurry

### Protective Clothing
**What you'll be provided:**
- **Clean uniforms** changed regularly
- **Hair covering** appropriate for your work area
- **Safety footwear** if required for your role
- **Protective aprons** for wet areas or chemical handling
- **Gloves** where specified for your tasks

**Your responsibility:**
- **Wear correctly** - all items properly fitted and secured
- **Keep clean** during your shift
- **Report damage** or problems immediately
- **Change when contaminated** or as instructed
- **Store appropriately** when not in use

## Hazard Analysis and Risk Assessment (HARA) System

### What Is HARA?
The HARA system is our systematic approach to:
- **Identify hazards** that could affect Product Safety
- **Assess the risk** of those hazards causing harm
- **Control hazards** through preventive measures
- **Monitor controls** to ensure they work effectively
- **Take corrective action** when controls fail

**BRCGS Requirement (2.1.1):** The HARA team must be competent and experienced in hazard analysis and risk assessment.

### Types of Hazards We Control
**Physical hazards:**
- **Foreign bodies** like metal, glass, or plastic pieces
- **Contamination** from equipment, maintenance, or personal items
- **Damage** to packaging that could allow contamination
- **Pests** that could contaminate materials or equipment

**Chemical hazards:**
- **Cleaning chemicals** that could remain on surfaces
- **Lubricants** from equipment that could contact packaging
- **Printing inks** applied incorrectly or with wrong formulation
- **Migration** of packaging components into food

**Biological hazards:**
- **Bacteria** from poor hygiene or contaminated surfaces
- **Mould** from damp conditions or poor cleaning
- **Cross-contamination** from one product to another
- **Illness transmission** from infected workers

### How HARA Protects You and Consumers
**Hazard prevention:**
- **Good manufacturing practices** prevent most hazards occurring
- **Equipment design** minimises contamination risks
- **Procedures** provide step-by-step hazard control
- **Training** ensures everyone knows how to prevent problems

**Risk control:**
- **Critical Control Points (CCPs)** for the most serious hazards
- **Monitoring systems** to detect problems quickly
- **Corrective actions** to fix problems immediately
- **Verification** that controls are working effectively

### Your Role in the HARA System
**Supporting hazard control:**
- **Follow procedures** that are designed to prevent hazards
- **Maintain hygiene** to prevent biological contamination
- **Handle materials carefully** to prevent physical contamination
- **Report problems** that could indicate hazard control failures
- **Ask questions** if you don't understand why procedures exist

**Being vigilant:**
- **Watch for hazards** throughout your work
- **Think about what could go wrong** in your activities
- **Report near-misses** that could have caused problems
- **Learn from incidents** to prevent them recurring
- **Suggest improvements** to make hazard control better

## Safety and Emergency Procedures

### Workplace Safety
**Your safety responsibilities:**
- **Follow safety procedures** for your work area
- **Use protective equipment** as required
- **Report hazards** that could cause injury
- **Keep walkways clear** to prevent trips and falls
- **Know emergency procedures** for your area

### Emergency Contacts
**Who to contact:**
- **Supervisor or Team Leader** for routine problems
- **Quality Manager** for product safety issues
- **Site Manager** for serious incidents
- **Emergency services (999)** for immediate danger
- **First Aid personnel** for injuries

### Fire and Evacuation
**If you hear the fire alarm:**
- **Stop work immediately** and secure equipment safely
- **Leave building** via nearest exit route
- **Go to assembly point** as shown on maps
- **Report to warden** for roll call
- **Don't return** until given all-clear

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **Name three personal hygiene requirements you must follow every day at Kangopak.**
   - Answer: Hand washing for 20 seconds, wear clean uniform, cover hair completely, no jewellery except wedding rings, don't eat in production areas (any three)

2. **What does HARA stand for and why is it important?**
   - Answer: Hazard Analysis and Risk Assessment - systematic approach to identify, assess, and control hazards that could affect Product Safety

3. **Who should you contact if you have a product safety concern during your first week?**
   - Answer: Your supervisor/team leader first, or Quality Manager for serious product safety issues

## Simple Practical Activity (15 minutes)

**Induction Tour and Practice:**

1. **Facility tour:** Walk through key areas (changing rooms, production, break areas)
2. **Hand washing practice:** Demonstrate proper hand washing technique
3. **Uniform fitting:** Try on and properly adjust protective clothing
4. **Emergency route:** Walk evacuation route to assembly point
5. **Contact practice:** Identify where emergency contact numbers are displayed
6. **Documentation sample:** Complete a simple record form correctly
7. **Question opportunity:** Ask any remaining questions about starting work

**Assessment:** Trainer confirms understanding of key safety and hygiene requirements.
