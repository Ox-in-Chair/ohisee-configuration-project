# Emergency Procedures and Response Training

**Module:** Emergency Procedures and Response  
**Audience:** All factory personnel  
**BRCGS Reference:** 4.4 Security, 6.1.1 Site Standards  
**Kangopak Procedure:** 4.5 Emergency Response Procedures

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

1. **Identify** different types of emergencies that may occur at Kangopak
2. **Respond** appropriately to emergency situations following established procedures
3. **Evacuate** safely and efficiently when required
4. **Report** emergencies correctly and provide assistance to emergency services

## Why Emergency Preparedness Matters

Proper emergency response protects the safety of personnel, minimises property damage, and ensures business continuity. Quick, correct responses can prevent minor incidents from becoming major disasters.

**Key BRCGS Requirement (4.4):** Sites must have appropriate emergency procedures to protect personnel and maintain security during crisis situations.

**Kangopak Standard:** All personnel must be trained in emergency response procedures and participate in regular emergency drills to maintain preparedness.

## Types of Emergencies

### Fire Emergencies
- **Small fires:** Localised fires that may be controlled with fire extinguishers
- **Major fires:** Large fires requiring evacuation and fire brigade response
- **Electrical fires:** Fires involving electrical equipment requiring special procedures
- **Chemical fires:** Fires involving cleaning chemicals or other hazardous materials
- **Prevention focus:** Most fires are preventable through good housekeeping and maintenance

### Medical Emergencies
- **Minor injuries:** Cuts, bruises, and minor accidents
- **Major injuries:** Serious accidents requiring immediate medical attention
- **Illness:** Sudden illness, heart attacks, or allergic reactions
- **Chemical exposure:** Contact with cleaning chemicals or other hazardous substances
- **First aid response:** Immediate care while waiting for professional medical help

### Security Emergencies
- **Unauthorised intruders:** Unknown persons on site without permission
- **Theft or vandalism:** Criminal activity affecting site security
- **Threatening behaviour:** Aggressive or violent behaviour by individuals
- **Bomb threats:** Reported explosive devices or threats
- **Civil unrest:** External events affecting site security

### Utility Emergencies
- **Power outages:** Loss of electrical supply affecting operations
- **Gas leaks:** Natural gas or LPG leaks requiring evacuation
- **Water supply issues:** Loss of water supply or contamination
- **Heating system failures:** Loss of heating during cold weather
- **Communication failures:** Loss of telephone or internet services

### Environmental Emergencies
- **Chemical spills:** Large spills requiring specialised cleanup
- **Severe weather:** Floods, storms, or extreme weather conditions
- **Environmental contamination:** Pollution incidents affecting surrounding area
- **Structural damage:** Building damage from weather or other causes
- **Transportation incidents:** Road accidents affecting site access

## Fire Emergency Procedures

### Fire Discovery Actions
1. **Raise the alarm:** Activate nearest fire alarm call point immediately
2. **Attempt firefighting:** Only if fire is small and you are trained to use extinguishers
3. **Evacuate if necessary:** Leave immediately if fire cannot be controlled
4. **Call emergency services:** Dial 999 for fire brigade if major fire
5. **Report to assembly point:** Go to designated assembly area and report to fire warden

### Fire Alarm Response
- **Stop work immediately:** Cease all activities when alarm sounds
- **Switch off equipment:** Turn off machinery if safely possible
- **Close doors:** Help contain fire spread by closing doors behind you
- **Use nearest exit:** Leave by shortest safe route to assembly point
- **Do not use lifts:** Use stairs only during fire evacuation

### Fire Fighting Equipment
- **Water extinguishers:** For paper, wood, and fabric fires (red label)
- **Foam extinguishers:** For flammable liquids (cream/yellow label)
- **CO2 extinguishers:** For electrical fires (black label)
- **Powder extinguishers:** For most fire types (blue label)
- **Fire blankets:** For small fires and person whose clothing is alight

## Medical Emergency Procedures

### Accident Response
1. **Ensure safety:** Make sure area is safe before helping injured person
2. **Assess situation:** Determine severity of injury and required response
3. **Provide first aid:** Give appropriate first aid within your competence level
4. **Get help:** Call for trained first aider or emergency services as needed
5. **Report incident:** Complete accident report and inform management

### Calling for Medical Help
- **Minor injuries:** Contact trained first aider on site
- **Serious injuries:** Call 999 for ambulance service immediately
- **Chemical exposure:** Call 999 and have safety data sheet available
- **Multiple casualties:** Call 999 and establish triage priorities
- **Information to provide:** Location, nature of injury, number of casualties, hazards present

### First Aid Response
- **Stay calm:** Keep calm to help injured person and others
- **Don't move seriously injured:** Avoid moving person unless in immediate danger
- **Control bleeding:** Apply direct pressure to wounds
- **Check breathing:** Ensure airway is clear and person is breathing
- **Treat for shock:** Keep person warm and comfortable

## Practical Factory Examples

### DO's ✓
- **Learn alarm sounds:** Know what different alarm signals mean
- **Practice evacuation:** Take part in fire drills seriously
- **Know your routes:** Be familiar with evacuation routes from your work area  
- **Report immediately:** Report emergencies and potential hazards promptly
- **Stay informed:** Listen for instructions from emergency wardens

### DON'Ts ✗
- **Never ignore alarms:** Always respond to emergency signals immediately
- **Never use lifts:** Use stairs only during evacuation
- **Never go back:** Don't return to building until given all-clear
- **Never block exits:** Keep evacuation routes clear at all times
- **Never panic:** Stay calm and follow established procedures

## Communication During Emergencies

### Internal Communications
- **PA system:** Listen for announcements over public address system
- **Two-way radios:** Management and wardens use radios for coordination
- **Mobile phones:** May be used if landline systems fail
- **Runners:** Personnel may be assigned to carry messages if communications fail
- **Visual signals:** Hand signals may be used in noisy environments

### External Communications
- **Emergency services:** 999 calls for police, fire, ambulance, or gas emergency
- **Utility companies:** Report gas, electricity, or water emergencies
- **Environmental agency:** Report pollution incidents or environmental hazards
- **Local authority:** Notify of incidents affecting public safety
- **Insurance companies:** Report significant incidents affecting property

### Information to Provide
- **Your name and position:** Identify yourself to emergency services
- **Location details:** Exact location of emergency within site
- **Nature of emergency:** Type and severity of incident
- **People involved:** Number of people affected or at risk
- **Hazards present:** Any special dangers emergency services should know about

## Shelter-in-Place Procedures

### When Evacuation Is Not Safe
- **Chemical releases:** External chemical incidents may require staying indoors
- **Severe weather:** Extreme weather conditions making evacuation dangerous
- **Security threats:** External threats where evacuation would increase risk
- **Utility failures:** Gas leaks or other utilities making outdoor areas dangerous

### Shelter-in-Place Actions
1. **Move to designated area:** Go to predetermined shelter area
2. **Seal room:** Close and tape windows, doors, and vents if chemical release
3. **Turn off ventilation:** Switch off air conditioning and heating systems
4. **Stay informed:** Listen to radio or other communications for updates
5. **Wait for all-clear:** Remain in shelter until officially notified it's safe to leave

## Post-Emergency Procedures

### After Evacuation
- **Remain at assembly point:** Stay until told it's safe to return or leave
- **Account for personnel:** Help wardens confirm all personnel are safe
- **Provide information:** Tell emergency services about any missing persons
- **Await instructions:** Wait for management instructions about returning to work
- **Debriefing:** Participate in post-emergency review and improvement activities

### Return to Normal Operations
- **All-clear signal:** Wait for official notification that it's safe to return
- **Damage assessment:** Management will assess building and equipment safety
- **Hazard clearance:** Ensure all hazards cleared before resuming work
- **Equipment checks:** Verify equipment is safe before restarting operations
- **Incident investigation:** Cooperate with investigation to prevent similar incidents

## Emergency Drills and Training

### Regular Drills
- **Fire drills:** Monthly fire evacuation exercises
- **Emergency response:** Quarterly testing of emergency procedures
- **First aid training:** Annual refresher training for first aiders
- **Communication tests:** Regular testing of emergency communication systems
- **Scenario exercises:** Periodic training using different emergency scenarios

### Drill Evaluation
- **Timing:** Record evacuation times and compare to targets
- **Completeness:** Verify all personnel participated and reached assembly points
- **Issues identification:** Note any problems or areas for improvement
- **Feedback:** Gather comments from participants about drill effectiveness
- **Improvements:** Update procedures based on drill results and feedback

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **What should you do when you hear the fire alarm?**
   - Answer: Stop work immediately, switch off equipment if safe, evacuate via nearest exit to assembly point

2. **When should you attempt to fight a fire with an extinguisher?**
   - Answer: Only if the fire is small, you are trained to use extinguishers, and you can evacuate safely if unsuccessful

3. **Who should you report to at the assembly point during evacuation?**
   - Answer: The designated fire warden or emergency coordinator for your area

4. **What information should you provide when calling emergency services?**
   - Answer: Your name, exact location, nature of emergency, number of people involved, and any special hazards

## Special Considerations

### Visitors and Contractors
- **Visitor responsibility:** Escort responsible for visitor safety during emergencies
- **Contractor briefing:** All contractors briefed on emergency procedures during induction
- **Assembly inclusion:** Visitors and contractors included in headcounts at assembly points
- **Language barriers:** Ensure emergency instructions understood by all personnel
- **Assistance needs:** Identify and help people who may need extra assistance

### Disabled Personnel
- **Individual plans:** Personal emergency evacuation plans for disabled personnel
- **Assistance arrangements:** Designated personnel to assist during evacuation
- **Equipment needs:** Special equipment such as evacuation chairs available
- **Alternative routes:** Accessible evacuation routes identified and maintained
- **Communication aids:** Visual and audible alarms for hearing or sight impaired
