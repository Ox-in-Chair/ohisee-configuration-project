# Employee Responsibilities Training

**Module:** Employee Responsibilities  
**Audience:** All personnel  
**BRCGS Reference:** 1.3.2  
**Kangopak Procedure:** 6.2 Product Safety Culture Procedure

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

1. **Describe** your specific responsibilities as a Kangopak employee for product safety and quality
2. **Demonstrate** accountability for your role in maintaining food packaging safety standards
3. **Explain** how your daily actions contribute to overall business success and customer satisfaction

## Why Employee Responsibilities Matter

Every person at Kangopak has important responsibilities for ensuring our packaging products are safe for food contact. Understanding your role and being accountable for your actions protects consumers, supports our customers, and maintains our reputation as a trusted supplier.

**Key BRCGS Requirement (1.3.2):** Personnel must be aware of their responsibilities and demonstrate accountability in their role.

**Kangopak Commitment:** Employee responsibility and accountability training is part of our annually reviewed product safety culture plan, ensuring everyone understands their contribution to our success.

## Core Employee Responsibilities

### Product Safety Responsibilities
**Every employee must:**
- **Follow all procedures** exactly as documented without shortcuts
- **Maintain hygiene standards** appropriate for food packaging production
- **Report problems immediately** that could affect product safety
- **Keep work areas clean** and organised to prevent contamination
- **Use equipment correctly** to maintain product quality and safety
- **Participate in training** to maintain knowledge and competency

### Quality Responsibilities
**Your role in quality:**
- **Check your work** against specifications and standards
- **Take pride in output** - every product represents Kangopak's reputation
- **Identify improvements** and suggest better ways of working
- **Support quality systems** by maintaining accurate records
- **Learn continuously** to improve your skills and knowledge

### Workplace Responsibilities
**Professional behaviour includes:**
- **Arrive on time** and ready to work safely and effectively
- **Communicate respectfully** with colleagues, supervisors, and visitors
- **Follow company policies** on attendance, conduct, and safety
- **Use company resources** appropriately and efficiently
- **Maintain confidentiality** of business and customer information

### Legal and Regulatory Responsibilities
**Compliance requirements:**
- **Follow Product Safety laws** that apply to packaging production
- **Adhere to health and safety** regulations for your protection and others
- **Maintain records** as required for traceability and compliance
- **Cooperate with inspections** by regulators and customers
- **Report violations** of legal or company requirements

## Understanding Accountability

### What Is Accountability?
Accountability means:
- **Taking responsibility** for your actions and decisions
- **Accepting consequences** when things go wrong
- **Learning from mistakes** to prevent them happening again
- **Following through** on commitments and assignments
- **Being reliable** so others can depend on you

### Personal Accountability in Practice
**Daily accountability:**
- **Own your mistakes** - don't blame others or make excuses
- **Ask for help** when you're unsure about procedures
- **Complete tasks properly** even when no one is watching
- **Speak up** about problems rather than hoping they'll go away
- **Keep promises** you make to colleagues and supervisors

### Team Accountability
**Supporting your team:**
- **Help colleagues** learn procedures and improve performance
- **Share information** that helps others do their jobs better
- **Take initiative** to solve problems rather than waiting for direction
- **Give constructive feedback** when you see opportunities for improvement
- **Celebrate successes** and learn from failures together

## Specific Role-Based Responsibilities

### Production Operators
**Core responsibilities:**
- **Follow work instructions** exactly for every product and process
- **Monitor quality** throughout production and report deviations
- **Maintain equipment** according to cleaning and maintenance schedules
- **Document activities** accurately and completely
- **Ensure material handling** maintains traceability and prevents contamination

### Material Handlers
**Key responsibilities:**
- **Verify specifications** of incoming materials against job requirements
- **Maintain storage conditions** to preserve material quality
- **Practice FIFO rotation** to prevent material deterioration
- **Handle materials carefully** to prevent damage or contamination
- **Maintain accurate inventory** records for traceability

### Quality Personnel
**Special responsibilities:**
- **Conduct tests** according to specified methods and frequencies
- **Report results** accurately and promptly
- **Investigate problems** thoroughly and objectively
- **Support improvement** activities with technical expertise
- **Maintain calibration** of measuring equipment

### Supervisors and Team Leaders
**Leadership responsibilities:**
- **Set good example** through your own behaviour and attitude
- **Support team development** through coaching and feedback
- **Ensure compliance** with procedures and requirements
- **Communicate effectively** between management and team members
- **Resolve problems** promptly and fairly

## Rights and Support Available

### Your Rights as an Employee
**What you can expect:**
- **Safe working environment** free from hazards and harassment
- **Proper training** to perform your job competently
- **Fair treatment** regardless of background or personal characteristics
- **Access to information** needed to do your job effectively
- **Opportunity to contribute** ideas for improvement

### Support Systems
**Help available when needed:**
- **Supervisors and managers** for guidance and problem-solving
- **Training department** for skills development and competency
- **HR department** for employment and welfare issues
- **Health and safety representatives** for safety concerns
- **Quality team** for technical and procedural questions

### Reporting Mechanisms
**How to raise concerns:**
- **Direct communication** with your supervisor or manager
- **Formal grievance procedures** for serious issues
- **Whistleblowing procedures** for regulatory or ethical concerns
- **Anonymous reporting** where appropriate systems exist
- **Employee representatives** for collective issues

## Consequences and Recognition

### Consequences of Not Meeting Responsibilities
**Progressive approach:**
- **Informal coaching** for minor issues or misunderstandings
- **Formal training** to address knowledge or skill gaps
- **Written warnings** for repeated failures to meet responsibilities
- **Disciplinary action** for serious breaches of responsibility
- **Dismissal** for gross misconduct or persistent failure

### Recognition for Excellence
**How good performance is recognised:**
- **Regular feedback** and appreciation from supervisors
- **Performance awards** for exceptional contribution
- **Development opportunities** for those who show initiative
- **Career advancement** based on demonstrated responsibility
- **Team recognition** for collective achievements

## Practical Factory Examples

### Demonstrating Responsibility ✓
- **Quality focus:** "I noticed this seal looked different from normal, so I stopped production and called Quality to check it"
- **Initiative:** "I saw the cleaning schedule wasn't being followed properly, so I discussed it with my supervisor and helped train the new person"
- **Accountability:** "I made a mistake with the temperature setting and reported it immediately so we could check the affected product"
- **Teamwork:** "I helped my colleague understand the new procedure so they could complete their training"

### Avoiding Responsibility ✗
- **Blame shifting:** "It's not my fault - someone else should have told me about the procedure change"
- **Ignoring problems:** "I saw something wasn't right but I didn't want to cause trouble"
- **Shortcuts:** "I skipped the cleaning step because we were running late"
- **Poor communication:** "I knew there was a problem but I didn't mention it to anyone"

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **What does personal accountability mean in your daily work?**
   - Answer: Taking responsibility for actions and decisions, owning mistakes, following through on commitments, being reliable, asking for help when needed

2. **Name three specific responsibilities that apply to all Kangopak employees.**
   - Answer: Follow procedures exactly, maintain hygiene standards, report problems immediately, check work quality, participate in training (any three)

3. **How does your role contribute to overall food packaging safety?**
   - Answer: Following procedures prevents contamination, quality checking prevents defective products reaching customers, reporting problems enables rapid response, maintaining standards protects consumer health

## Simple Practical Activity (10 minutes)

**Responsibility Scenarios:**

1. **Scenario cards:** Trainer presents workplace situations requiring responsible action
2. **Individual response:** Think about what you would do in each situation
3. **Group discussion:** Compare responses and identify responsible approaches
4. **Role play:** Practice difficult conversations about responsibility
5. **Personal reflection:** Consider how you demonstrate responsibility in your current role

**Example scenarios:**
- You notice a colleague consistently skipping a safety step
- Equipment is malfunctioning but production target is pressing
- You make a mistake that could affect product quality
- A customer complains about something related to your work

## What Supervisors Look For

Your supervisor will assess:

- **Ownership attitude** - taking responsibility for your work area and outputs
- **Proactive behaviour** - identifying and addressing problems before they escalate
- **Reliable performance** - consistent execution of duties regardless of circumstances
- **Learning mindset** - seeking to understand and improve your contribution
- **Team contribution** - supporting colleagues and overall team objectives
- **Professional conduct** - representing Kangopak values in all interactions

**Regular feedback:** Supervisors provide ongoing feedback on how well you're meeting your responsibilities and areas for development.
