# Safe Use of Electric Tools Training

**Module:** Safe Use of Electric Tools  
**Audience:** Maintenance technicians and authorised personnel  
**BRCGS Reference:** 6.1.2, 4.7 (Maintenance)  
**Kangopak Procedure:** 4.3 BRCGS Maintenance of Plant and Equipment

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

1. **Identify** different types of electric tools and their safety requirements
2. **Perform** proper safety checks before using electric tools
3. **Use** electric tools safely to prevent injury and product contamination
4. **Report** defects and maintenance requirements correctly

## Why Electric Tool Safety Matters

Electric tools are powerful and potentially dangerous equipment. They can cause severe injury or death if used incorrectly. Proper training and safety procedures protect both workers and products from harm.

**Key BRCGS Requirement (6.1.2):** Electric tools must be used only by trained personnel with proper safety measures in place.

**Kangopak Standard:** All electric tools are dangerous equipment requiring specific training, safety procedures, and regular inspection by qualified personnel.

## Types of Electric Tools at Kangopak

### Workshop Electric Tools
- **Power drills:** For maintenance and repair work
- **Angle grinders:** Metal cutting and surface preparation
- **Electric saws:** Cutting various materials
- **Sanders:** Surface preparation and finishing
- **Impact wrenches:** High-torque fastening operations

### Portable Electric Tools
- **Handheld drills:** Light drilling operations
- **Electric screwdrivers:** Assembly and maintenance
- **Heat guns:** Material heating and adhesive removal
- **Soldering irons:** Electrical repair work
- **Multi-tools:** Various cutting and shaping operations

### Tool Location and Control
- **Designated storage:** Workshop locations for most tools
- **Operations Manager:** Some tools kept with Operations Manager
- **Tool Inspector:** Appointed person responsible for tool management
- **Inventory control:** All tools tracked and registered

## Pre-Use Safety Inspections

### Visual Inspection Checklist
1. **Power cord:** Check for cuts, fraying, or damage
2. **Plug condition:** Ensure pins are secure and undamaged
3. **Tool housing:** Look for cracks or damage to case
4. **Guards and shields:** Verify all safety guards are in place
5. **Moving parts:** Check for looseness or excessive wear
6. **Switches:** Test on/off controls function properly

### Functional Testing
- **Power-on test:** Verify tool starts and runs smoothly
- **Speed control:** Check variable speed controls work correctly
- **Safety features:** Test emergency stops and safety interlocks
- **Vibration check:** Excessive vibration indicates problems
- **Noise assessment:** Unusual noises suggest internal damage

### Documentation Requirements
- **Monthly inspections:** All tools inspected and recorded monthly
- **Inspection register:** Record kept of all inspections and findings
- **Defect reporting:** Faulty tools recorded in deviation register
- **Out-of-service tagging:** Defective tools marked and removed from use

## Safe Operating Procedures

### Before Starting Work
1. **Select correct tool:** Use the right tool for the specific job
2. **Inspect thoroughly:** Complete visual and functional checks
3. **Wear PPE:** Appropriate personal protective equipment required
4. **Clear work area:** Remove unnecessary materials and ensure adequate space
5. **Plan the work:** Consider sequence of operations and safety requirements

### During Operation
1. **Maintain control:** Keep firm grip and stable stance
2. **Guard positioning:** Never remove or disable safety guards
3. **Cutting direction:** Cut away from body and other personnel
4. **Avoid overreach:** Maintain balance and proper positioning
5. **Take breaks:** Avoid fatigue from extended use

### After Use
1. **Switch off properly:** Turn off tool and wait for complete stop
2. **Clean thoroughly:** Remove debris and product residue
3. **Inspect for damage:** Check for any damage that occurred during use
4. **Store correctly:** Return to designated storage location
5. **Report issues:** Inform Electric Tool Inspector of any problems

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

### Required PPE for Electric Tools
- **Safety glasses:** Eye protection from debris and sparks
- **Hearing protection:** Ear defenders for noisy tools
- **Work gloves:** Cut-resistant gloves for handling materials
- **Safety boots:** Steel-toed boots for foot protection
- **Dust masks:** Respiratory protection when generating dust

### Specialised PPE
- **Face shields:** Full face protection for grinding operations
- **Welding masks:** Eye protection for welding and cutting
- **Heat-resistant gloves:** Protection from hot materials and tools
- **Aprons:** Body protection from sparks and debris
- **Hard hats:** Head protection in areas with overhead hazards

## Debris and Contamination Control

### Types of Debris from Electric Tools
- **Metal shavings:** From drilling and cutting operations
- **Grinding dust:** Particles from surface preparation
- **Broken bits:** Drill bits, saw blades, and cutting tools
- **Drilling oils:** Lubricants and cutting fluids
- **Worn components:** Rags, brushes, and worn-out parts

### Contamination Prevention
1. **Work area preparation:** Isolate work from production areas
2. **Debris collection:** Use proper collection methods and containers
3. **Clean as you go:** Remove debris immediately during work
4. **Final cleanup:** Thorough cleaning before returning to production
5. **Inspection:** Check for any remaining contamination sources

### Product Safety Measures
- **Physical barriers:** Separate maintenance work from production
- **Scheduling:** Plan maintenance during non-production periods
- **Air flow control:** Consider ventilation to prevent debris spread
- **Final inspection:** Verify no contamination before production resumes

## Practical Factory Examples

### DO's ✓
- **Use correct PPE:** Always wear appropriate safety equipment
- **Inspect before use:** Complete thorough pre-use inspections
- **Maintain guards:** Keep all safety guards in proper position
- **Work systematically:** Plan operations for maximum safety
- **Clean thoroughly:** Remove all debris and contamination after use

### DON'Ts ✗
- **Never rush:** Take time to work safely and methodically
- **Never disable guards:** Safety guards must remain in place and functional
- **Never use damaged tools:** Stop work if tools show any defects
- **Never work alone:** Have supervision available, especially for complex operations
- **Never ignore safety procedures:** Always follow established protocols

## Common Electric Tool Hazards

### Electrical Hazards
- **Electric shock:** From damaged cords or wet conditions
- **Short circuits:** Caused by damaged wiring or connections
- **Fire risk:** From sparks in flammable environments
- **Electrocution:** Severe electrical contact can be fatal

### Mechanical Hazards
- **Cuts and lacerations:** From sharp blades and bits
- **Impact injuries:** From kickback or tool malfunction
- **Flying debris:** Particles and fragments from cutting operations
- **Crushing injuries:** From heavy tools or materials

### Environmental Hazards
- **Noise exposure:** Hearing damage from loud tools
- **Dust inhalation:** Respiratory problems from airborne particles
- **Vibration effects:** Hand and arm disorders from prolonged use
- **Heat exposure:** Burns from hot tools and materials

## Emergency Procedures

### If Someone Is Injured
1. **Stop work immediately:** Turn off tools and secure area
2. **Provide first aid:** Apply appropriate immediate care
3. **Call for help:** Contact medical assistance if needed
4. **Report incident:** Notify supervisor and complete incident report
5. **Preserve evidence:** Don't disturb area until investigation complete

### If Tools Malfunction
1. **Stop use immediately:** Turn off and disconnect power
2. **Secure area:** Prevent others from using defective tool
3. **Tag out of service:** Mark tool as defective and unusable
4. **Report to inspector:** Inform Electric Tool Inspector immediately
5. **Complete documentation:** Record defect in deviation register

## Tool Maintenance and Inspection

### Electric Tool Inspector Responsibilities
- **Maintain tool inventory:** Keep accurate records of all tools
- **Conduct inspections:** Monthly inspection of all electric tools
- **Ensure safe storage:** Tools kept in designated, secure locations
- **Coordinate repairs:** Arrange repair or replacement of defective tools
- **Monitor training:** Ensure only trained personnel use tools

### Monthly Inspection Requirements
- **Visual examination:** Check for damage, wear, or deterioration
- **Functional testing:** Verify proper operation and safety features
- **Record keeping:** Document inspection results on registers
- **Defect action:** Remove defective tools and arrange repairs
- **Compliance verification:** Ensure adherence to maintenance procedures

### Repair and Replacement
- **Qualified technicians:** Only trained personnel perform repairs
- **Genuine parts:** Use manufacturer-approved replacement parts
- **Testing after repair:** Verify proper function before return to service
- **Documentation:** Record all repairs and modifications
- **Disposal:** Properly dispose of tools that cannot be repaired

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **What must you do before using any electric tool?**
   - Answer: Complete visual inspection, functional testing, and wear appropriate PPE

2. **Who is responsible for monthly electric tool inspections?**
   - Answer: The appointed Electric Tool Inspector

3. **What should you do if you discover a defective electric tool?**
   - Answer: Stop use, tag as "OUT OF SERVICE", and report to Electric Tool Inspector immediately

4. **What types of debris must be controlled when using electric tools?**
   - Answer: Metal shavings, grinding dust, broken bits, drilling oils, and worn components
