# Product Safety Responsibilities Training

**Module:** Product Safety Responsibilities  
**Audience:** All personnel  
**BRCGS Reference:** 1.1.2, 1.3.2  
**Kangopak Procedure:** 1.1 Product Safety Culture Procedure

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

1. **Identify** your specific product safety responsibilities for your role at Kangopak
2. **Understand** how product safety responsibilities apply to different departments and functions
3. **Implement** daily practices that support product safety throughout our operations

## Why Product Safety Responsibilities Are Critical

Product safety responsibilities ensure that everyone at Kangopak understands their specific role in protecting consumers who use food packaged in our products. Clear responsibilities prevent gaps in our safety systems and ensure coordinated effort across all departments.

**Key BRCGS Requirements:**
- 1.1.2: Product safety and quality culture development
- 1.3.2: Personnel awareness of their responsibilities

**Kangopak Commitment:** Understanding respective product safety-related responsibilities for each employee and department is part of our annually reviewed product safety culture plan.

## Understanding Product Safety Responsibilities

### What Are Product Safety Responsibilities?
Product safety responsibilities are specific duties assigned to individuals and departments to:
- **Prevent contamination** of packaging materials during production
- **Control hazards** that could make packaging unsafe for food contact
- **Maintain systems** that protect product integrity throughout our processes
- **Respond effectively** when safety problems are identified
- **Support compliance** with regulatory and customer requirements

### Department-Specific Responsibilities
Each department has unique responsibilities while supporting overall product safety:

**Production:** Direct control over product safety during manufacturing
**Quality:** Systems for monitoring and verifying safety controls
**Maintenance:** Equipment integrity to prevent contamination sources
**Warehousing:** Material protection during storage and handling
**Administration:** Documentation and communication support

### Individual vs Collective Responsibilities
**Individual responsibilities:** What you personally must do in your role
**Collective responsibilities:** What we must achieve together as teams
**Shared accountability:** Everyone contributing to the same safety outcomes

## Production Department Responsibilities

### Operators and Production Staff
**Primary responsibilities:**
- **Follow all procedures** exactly as documented without deviation
- **Monitor processes** continuously for signs of problems or deviations
- **Maintain equipment** according to cleaning and maintenance schedules
- **Handle materials** in ways that prevent contamination or damage
- **Report problems** immediately to appropriate personnel
- **Complete documentation** accurately and completely

**Specific safety duties:**
- **Check material specifications** before starting production
- **Monitor critical control points** where assigned
- **Perform quality checks** during production processes
- **Maintain work area cleanliness** to prevent contamination sources
- **Use personal protective equipment** correctly and consistently

### Production Supervisors
**Leadership responsibilities:**
- **Ensure team compliance** with all safety procedures
- **Provide guidance** when problems or unusual situations occur
- **Coordinate responses** to safety issues or non-conformances
- **Support training** and competency development of team members
- **Interface with other departments** on product safety matters

**System responsibilities:**
- **Review production records** for completeness and accuracy
- **Investigate deviations** from normal process parameters
- **Authorise corrective actions** within their scope of authority
- **Escalate problems** that require higher-level decision making
- **Maintain communication** with Quality and Management teams

## Quality Department Responsibilities

### Quality Technicians
**Testing and verification:**
- **Conduct tests** according to specified methods and frequencies
- **Maintain measuring equipment** in calibrated condition
- **Document results** accurately and promptly
- **Compare results** to specifications and acceptance criteria
- **Report non-conformances** following documented procedures

**System support:**
- **Review production documentation** for completeness
- **Support investigations** of product safety issues
- **Maintain calibration records** and schedules
- **Interface with customers** on technical and safety matters
- **Support internal audits** and external assessments

### Quality Manager
**Overall system responsibility:**
- **Design and maintain** quality and safety management systems
- **Ensure regulatory compliance** for all product safety requirements
- **Manage customer relationships** regarding quality and safety issues
- **Lead investigations** of serious product safety problems
- **Report to management** on system performance and improvements needed

## Maintenance Department Responsibilities

### Maintenance Technicians
**Equipment integrity:**
- **Maintain equipment** to prevent contamination from wear or malfunction
- **Use appropriate materials** that don't pose contamination risks
- **Clean up properly** after maintenance activities
- **Report potential problems** before they affect product safety
- **Support installation** of new equipment with safety considerations

**Documentation duties:**
- **Record maintenance activities** with sufficient detail for traceability
- **Update equipment records** when modifications are made
- **Maintain spare parts** in appropriate storage conditions
- **Support calibration** activities for measuring equipment

### Maintenance Supervisor
**System oversight:**
- **Plan maintenance** to minimise product safety risks
- **Ensure technician training** on product safety requirements
- **Coordinate with production** to minimise operational disruptions
- **Manage contractors** working on site to ensure they understand safety requirements
- **Review maintenance records** for completeness and accuracy

## Warehousing and Logistics Responsibilities

### Warehouse Staff
**Material protection:**
- **Handle materials** carefully to prevent damage or contamination
- **Maintain storage conditions** according to specifications
- **Practice FIFO rotation** to prevent material deterioration
- **Keep storage areas clean** and free from contamination sources
- **Report damage** or quality issues immediately

**Documentation accuracy:**
- **Maintain accurate inventory** records for traceability
- **Complete receiving documentation** properly
- **Record storage conditions** where required
- **Support stock investigations** when quality issues arise

### Logistics Coordinators
**Distribution safety:**
- **Arrange appropriate transport** that maintains product integrity
- **Communicate requirements** to transport providers
- **Track shipments** to ensure timely delivery
- **Handle customer queries** about deliveries and product status
- **Support product recalls** if required

## Administrative Support Responsibilities

### Document Control
**System maintenance:**
- **Maintain current procedures** and work instructions
- **Distribute updates** effectively to all users
- **Archive superseded documents** appropriately
- **Support training** with current documentation
- **Maintain records** of document changes and approvals

### Human Resources
**Personnel support:**
- **Ensure proper training** before personnel start work
- **Maintain training records** for compliance purposes
- **Support competency assessment** processes
- **Handle disciplinary issues** related to product safety failures
- **Support recruitment** with appropriate screening for safety-critical roles

### Management Team
**Overall accountability:**
- **Provide leadership** and resources for product safety
- **Review system performance** regularly and drive improvements
- **Ensure regulatory compliance** across all operations
- **Manage customer relationships** including safety requirements
- **Support culture development** that prioritises product safety

## Shared Responsibilities

### Communication
**Everyone must:**
- **Share information** that could affect product safety
- **Listen actively** to concerns raised by others
- **Ask questions** when requirements are unclear
- **Provide feedback** on system effectiveness
- **Support improvement** initiatives based on lessons learned

### Problem Recognition
**Universal vigilance:**
- **Watch for problems** that could affect product safety
- **Report concerns** promptly through appropriate channels
- **Don't ignore issues** hoping someone else will deal with them
- **Follow through** to ensure problems are resolved
- **Learn from incidents** to prevent recurrence

### Continuous Improvement
**Collective commitment:**
- **Suggest improvements** based on practical experience
- **Participate actively** in improvement projects
- **Share best practices** with colleagues
- **Support implementation** of new or changed procedures
- **Measure progress** and celebrate achievements

## Accountability Framework

### Individual Accountability
**What you're accountable for:**
- **Your own actions** and decisions affecting product safety
- **Following procedures** assigned to your role
- **Reporting problems** you discover or cause
- **Maintaining competency** through training and practice
- **Supporting team objectives** for product safety

### Team Accountability
**Collective responsibility:**
- **Team performance** in meeting safety objectives
- **Supporting colleagues** to succeed in their safety responsibilities
- **Identifying team-level improvements** needed
- **Communicating effectively** within and between teams
- **Escalating problems** that require higher-level attention

### Management Accountability
**Leadership responsibilities:**
- **Providing resources** needed for product safety
- **Setting clear expectations** for performance
- **Monitoring system effectiveness** and driving improvements
- **Ensuring competency** of all personnel
- **Reporting performance** to senior management and customers

## Practical Factory Examples

### Good Product Safety Responsibility ✓
- **Production operator:** "I noticed the heat seal temperature was trending downward, so I checked calibration and reported it before it went outside limits"
- **Quality technician:** "The test results showed a trend toward specification limits, so I increased monitoring frequency and notified production"
- **Maintenance technician:** "I found metal filings near the cutting blade during maintenance, so I cleaned thoroughly and checked recent production"
- **Warehouse staff:** "The incoming material looked different from normal, so I held it for Quality inspection before putting it into stock"

### Poor Product Safety Responsibility ✗
- **Ignoring problems:** "I saw something wasn't right but it wasn't my job to fix it"
- **Incomplete actions:** "I reported the problem but didn't follow up to see if it was resolved"
- **Working outside procedures:** "I knew the procedure but I thought my way would be better"
- **Poor communication:** "I assumed someone else would take care of it"

## Emergency Responsibilities

### When Product Safety Problems Occur
**Immediate response duties:**
- **Stop further production** if safety could be affected
- **Secure affected products** to prevent distribution
- **Notify appropriate personnel** according to escalation procedures
- **Document the situation** with sufficient detail for investigation
- **Support investigation** by providing accurate information

### Supporting Incident Response
**Everyone's role:**
- **Cooperate fully** with investigations
- **Provide factual information** about what you observed
- **Don't speculate** about causes or blame others
- **Learn from incidents** to improve future performance
- **Support corrective actions** required from the investigation

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **What are three product safety responsibilities that apply to all employees regardless of department?**
   - Answer: Report problems immediately, follow procedures exactly, maintain work area cleanliness, support investigations, participate in training (any three)

2. **How do production and quality department responsibilities differ for product safety?**
   - Answer: Production directly controls processes that affect safety; Quality monitors and verifies that safety controls are working effectively

3. **Why is it important that everyone understands their specific product safety responsibilities?**
   - Answer: Prevents gaps in safety systems, ensures coordinated effort, enables quick response to problems, supports regulatory compliance

## Simple Practical Activity (12 minutes)

**Responsibility Mapping Exercise:**

1. **Role identification:** Each participant identifies their current role and department
2. **Responsibility listing:** List 5 specific product safety responsibilities for your role
3. **Interface mapping:** Identify which other roles you interact with on product safety matters
4. **Scenario response:** How would you handle a product safety issue in your area?
5. **Gap analysis:** Are there any unclear responsibilities in your role?
6. **Improvement ideas:** Suggest ways to strengthen product safety responsibilities

**Group sharing:** Compare responsibilities across different roles and identify common themes.

## What Supervisors Look For

Your supervisor will assess:

- **Understanding clarity** - clear comprehension of your specific product safety responsibilities
- **Consistent implementation** - reliable execution of safety responsibilities throughout your work
- **Proactive behaviour** - identifying and addressing safety issues before they escalate
- **Communication effectiveness** - appropriate reporting and information sharing on safety matters
- **Team collaboration** - supporting colleagues in meeting their safety responsibilities
- **Continuous improvement** - contributing ideas and feedback to strengthen safety systems

**Regular review:** Product safety responsibilities are reviewed annually as part of culture development planning.
