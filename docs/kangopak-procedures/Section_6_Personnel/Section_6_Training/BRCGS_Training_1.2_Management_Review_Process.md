# Management Review Process Training

**Module:** Management Review Process  
**Audience:** Senior management (4 people)  
**BRCGS Reference:** 1.2.1  
**Kangopak Procedure:** 1.2 BRC Communications Procedure Rev 4

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

1. **Conduct** effective management review meetings that meet BRCGS requirements and drive continuous improvement
2. **Evaluate** the organisation's performance against BRCGS standards and strategic objectives systematically
3. **Implement** action plans resulting from management review with clear accountability and timelines
4. **Demonstrate** senior management commitment through structured review processes

## What Is Management Review?

Management review is a systematic evaluation of the organisation's performance against BRCGS standards and strategic objectives, conducted annually by senior management. It provides the foundation for continuous improvement and demonstrates leadership commitment to product safety and quality.

**Key BRCGS Requirement (1.2.1):** Management review meetings attended by the site's senior management shall be undertaken at appropriate scheduled intervals (at a minimum annually) to review the site's performance against the Standard and the objectives set out in clause 1.1.4.

**Kangopak Implementation:** Annual Management Review meetings documented through Management Review minutes (1.4F1) with structured agenda and action planning.

## Management Review Framework

### Strategic Performance Review
**Comprehensive assessment areas:**
- **BRCGS certification status** - maintaining Grade A performance
- **Product safety objectives** - achievement against annual targets
- **Quality system effectiveness** - customer satisfaction and compliance
- **Risk management performance** - hazard control and incident prevention
- **Continuous improvement** - innovation and operational excellence

### Systematic Data Analysis
**Evidence-based review using:**
- **Previous management review** documents, action plans and progress
- **Internal audit results** - compliance findings and improvement opportunities
- **External audit outcomes** - BRCGS, customer and regulatory assessments
- **Customer performance** indicators, complaints and feedback trends
- **HACCP effectiveness** - hazard analysis and risk assessment performance
- **Legislative changes** - impact of regulatory updates on operations

## Annual Management Review Structure

### Pre-Meeting Preparation
**Essential preparation activities:**
- **Performance data compilation** - collect metrics from all departments
- **Action plan review** - assess completion of previous year's commitments
- **Stakeholder input** - gather feedback from department heads and teams
- **External intelligence** - regulatory changes, industry trends, customer requirements
- **Resource assessment** - evaluate current capabilities against future needs

### Meeting Conduct
**Structured review process:**
- **Opening assessment** - overall performance against strategic objectives
- **Department presentations** - quality, production, maintenance, HR performance
- **Risk evaluation** - current hazards, control effectiveness, emerging threats
- **Customer analysis** - satisfaction trends, complaints, new requirements
- **Compliance review** - regulatory adherence, certification status, audit findings

### Decision Making and Planning
**Strategic planning outcomes:**
- **Objective setting** - establish measurable goals for coming year
- **Resource allocation** - approve budgets, personnel and equipment needs
- **Action planning** - assign specific responsibilities with clear deadlines
- **Risk mitigation** - approve controls for identified significant risks
- **Investment decisions** - capital expenditure for safety and quality improvements

## Key Review Topics

### BRCGS Performance Assessment
**Systematic evaluation areas:**
- **Certification grade** maintenance and improvement opportunities
- **Non-conformity trends** - root causes and preventive actions
- **Audit effectiveness** - internal audit program performance
- **Training compliance** - personnel competency and development needs
- **Document control** - procedure effectiveness and update requirements

### Product Safety and Quality
**Performance measurement:**
- **Customer complaints** analysis and resolution effectiveness
- **Quality metrics** - defect rates, rework, customer returns
- **Product recall/withdrawal** preparedness and incident history
- **Traceability system** performance and verification results
- **Supplier performance** - quality, delivery, compliance metrics

### Operational Effectiveness
**System performance review:**
- **HACCP system** effectiveness and validation results
- **Prerequisite programmes** - cleaning, pest control, maintenance performance
- **Equipment reliability** - breakdown rates, planned maintenance effectiveness
- **Process control** - stability, capability, improvement opportunities
- **Resource utilisation** - efficiency, capacity, productivity trends

### Legal and Regulatory Compliance
**Compliance assessment:**
- **Regulatory changes** impact on operations and compliance status
- **Legal requirements** adherence across all applicable legislation
- **Industry standards** alignment with best practices and benchmarks
- **Customer requirements** compliance with specific contractual obligations
- **Certification maintenance** ongoing requirements for standards compliance

## Practical Factory Examples

### Effective Management Review Practices ✓
- **Data-driven decisions:** "Quality metrics show 15% reduction in customer complaints following new training programme"
- **Strategic planning:** "Investment in automated inspection equipment will reduce physical contamination risk by 60%"
- **Accountability:** "Production Manager to implement enhanced CCP monitoring by March 31st with monthly progress reports"
- **Continuous improvement:** "Root cause analysis reveals training gaps - implement quarterly competency assessments"
- **Customer focus:** "New customer requirements for allergen labelling require procedure updates by June 1st"

### Ineffective Management Review Practices ✗
- **Superficial review:** "Everything looks fine - same objectives as last year"
- **Lack of data:** "We think quality is improving but don't have specific metrics"
- **No accountability:** "Someone should look into the complaint trends"
- **Reactive approach:** "We'll deal with problems when they come up"
- **Missing follow-up:** "Last year's action plans? We never got around to checking those"

## Management Review Outcomes

### Strategic Objectives Setting
**SMART objectives development:**
- **Specific** targets linked to BRCGS requirements and business goals
- **Measurable** metrics with clear success criteria and milestones
- **Achievable** goals considering available resources and constraints
- **Relevant** alignment with customer needs and regulatory requirements
- **Time-bound** delivery dates with intermediate review checkpoints

### Resource Planning
**Comprehensive resource assessment:**
- **Human resources** - competency development, recruitment, training needs
- **Financial resources** - budget allocation for quality and safety initiatives
- **Technical resources** - equipment, technology, facility improvements
- **Information resources** - data systems, documentation, communication tools
- **External resources** - consultant expertise, supplier capabilities, partnerships

### Risk Management
**Proactive risk approach:**
- **Emerging risks** identification and assessment protocols
- **Control measures** validation and improvement requirements
- **Contingency planning** for potential disruptions or incidents
- **Insurance coverage** adequacy and gap analysis
- **Business continuity** planning and crisis management preparedness

### Performance Monitoring
**Ongoing oversight systems:**
- **Dashboard development** for key performance indicators
- **Review frequency** - monthly, quarterly, annual assessment cycles
- **Escalation procedures** for performance deviations or emerging issues
- **Communication protocols** - reporting structures and stakeholder updates
- **Corrective actions** - systematic response to performance gaps

## Management Commitment Demonstration

### Leadership Behaviours
**Visible commitment through:**
- **Personal participation** in management review and follow-up activities
- **Resource provision** - adequate budget, personnel, time allocation
- **Decision authority** - empowering teams to implement improvements
- **Communication** - clear messaging about safety and quality priorities
- **Recognition** - celebrating achievements and learning from challenges

### Cultural Development
**Building safety-first culture:**
- **Vision alignment** - connecting individual roles to organisational mission
- **Value reinforcement** - consistent messaging about quality expectations
- **Behaviour modeling** - leadership demonstrating desired practices
- **Employee engagement** - encouraging participation and feedback
- **Continuous learning** - promoting innovation and improvement mindset

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **What are the minimum requirements for BRCGS management review meetings?**
   - Answer: Attended by senior management, conducted at minimum annually, review performance against the Standard and objectives set in clause 1.1.4

2. **Name five key data sources that should be reviewed during management review.**
   - Answer: Previous management review documents, internal/external audit results, customer performance indicators, HACCP effectiveness, legislative changes

3. **How should management review outcomes be implemented effectively?**
   - Answer: Through SMART objectives, clear accountability assignments, adequate resource allocation, defined timelines, and regular progress monitoring

## What Senior Leaders Must Demonstrate

Management review effectiveness requires:

- **Strategic thinking** - connecting operational performance to business objectives
- **Data analysis** - interpreting trends and identifying improvement opportunities
- **Decision-making** - making tough choices about resource allocation and priorities
- **Communication** - articulating vision and expectations clearly throughout organisation
- **Accountability** - following through on commitments and holding others accountable
- **Continuous improvement** - fostering innovation and learning from both successes and failures

**Leadership commitment:** Your active participation and follow-through demonstrates to the entire organisation that product safety and quality are genuine priorities, not just compliance requirements.
