# Product Safety Culture Training

**Module:** Product Safety Culture  
**Audience:** All factory personnel  
**BRCGS Reference:** 1.1.2  
**Kangopak Procedure:** 1.1.1 Product Safety Culture Procedure

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

1. **Understand** what product safety culture means in practical terms at Kangopak
2. **Identify** how your daily actions contribute to the safety of packaging that holds people's food
3. **Demonstrate** the values and behaviours that support our commitment to product safety excellence

## What Is Product Safety Culture?

Product safety culture is about everyone at Kangopak sharing the same commitment: making sure the packaging we produce is safe for the food that goes inside it. It's not just about following rules - it's about caring that families can trust the food in our packaging.

**Key BRCGS Requirement (1.1.2):** Senior management must define and maintain a plan for developing and improving product safety and quality culture, reviewed annually.

**Kangopak Commitment:** Our culture plan includes training activities reviewed annually at Management Review to ensure continuous improvement.

## Core Values of Our Safety Culture

### 1. Safety First, Always
- **Every decision** considers product safety impact first
- **No shortcuts** that could compromise product safety
- **Prevention focus** - stop problems before they happen
- **Continuous vigilance** throughout every shift

### 2. Personal Responsibility
- **Everyone owns safety** - from the factory floor to senior management
- **Speak up** when you see something that doesn't look right
- **Take action** within your authority to prevent problems
- **Learn continuously** to improve your knowledge and skills

### 3. Open Communication
- **No blame culture** - focus on fixing problems, not finding fault
- **Share information** that helps others work safely
- **Ask questions** if you're not sure about something
- **Listen actively** to colleagues' concerns and suggestions

### 4. Customer Focus
- **Remember the end user** - families eating food from our packaging
- **Pride in quality** - our packaging represents our reputation
- **Continuous improvement** based on customer needs and feedback
- **Reliability** - customers depend on us to get it right every time

## How Culture Shows in Daily Actions

### At the Start of Each Shift
- **Equipment checks** - verify everything is working correctly
- **Cleanliness inspection** - ensure work area meets hygiene standards
- **Material verification** - check raw materials meet specifications
- **Team communication** - share any issues or concerns from previous shift

### During Production
- **Constant monitoring** - watch for anything unusual or different
- **Quality consciousness** - check your work meets requirements
- **Problem reporting** - don't ignore small issues that could become big ones
- **Teamwork** - help colleagues maintain standards

### At the End of Shift
- **Proper cleanup** - leave work area ready for next team
- **Handover communication** - pass on important information
- **Equipment shutdown** - follow procedures to prevent contamination
- **Reflection** - think about what went well and what could improve

## Why Product Safety Culture Matters

### For Our Customers
- **Trust** in Kangopak packaging to keep their food safe
- **Confidence** that we follow the highest standards
- **Reliability** in meeting their specifications and delivery needs
- **Partnership** in their own product safety commitments

### For Consumers
- **Safe product packaging** that doesn't introduce harmful substances
- **Quality assurance** that packaging performs as expected
- **Health protection** through contamination prevention
- **Peace of mind** for families choosing products in our packaging

### For Kangopak
- **Reputation** as a trusted packaging supplier
- **Business growth** through customer satisfaction and retention
- **BRCGS certification** maintenance for market access
- **Job security** through sustainable business success

## Practical Factory Examples

### Strong Safety Culture Behaviours ✓
- **Proactive checking:** "I noticed this seam looks different - let me check the specification"
- **Open communication:** "I'm not sure about this procedure - can you show me the correct way?"
- **Problem ownership:** "I found this issue and here's what I did to fix it"
- **Continuous learning:** "I attended training and learned something that will help our team"
- **Customer thinking:** "Would I be happy if my family's food came in this packaging?"

### Weak Safety Culture Behaviours ✗
- **Assumption making:** "This small defect probably won't matter"
- **Blame focusing:** "It's not my fault - someone else should have caught that"
- **Rule shortcuts:** "We don't have time to follow the full procedure today"
- **Information hoarding:** "I know there's a problem but I won't say anything"
- **Task focus only:** "I just do my job and don't worry about the bigger picture"

## Building Stronger Safety Culture

### Individual Actions
- **Be curious** - ask questions about why procedures exist
- **Stay informed** - read notices, attend training, learn about product safety
- **Speak up** - share ideas for improvements
- **Take ownership** - don't wait for others to fix problems you can solve
- **Coach others** - help new team members learn our standards

### Team Actions
- **Regular discussions** about safety and quality in team meetings
- **Shared problem solving** when issues arise
- **Recognition** of good safety behaviours
- **Learning from mistakes** without blame
- **Celebrating successes** in safety performance

## Checkpoint Questions

Test your understanding with these questions:

1. **What does product safety culture mean in practical terms at Kangopak?**
   - Answer: Everyone sharing commitment to making packaging safe for food, caring about families who will use products in our packaging

2. **Name three behaviours that demonstrate strong safety culture.**
   - Answer: Speaking up about problems, checking work quality, asking questions when unsure, taking personal responsibility

3. **How does our safety culture benefit customers and consumers?**
   - Answer: Builds trust, ensures safe product packaging, prevents contamination, provides reliability and peace of mind

## Simple Practical Activity (10 minutes)

**Culture Assessment Exercise:**

1. **Small groups** discuss a recent work situation where safety was important
2. **Identify actions** that showed good safety culture
3. **Discuss improvements** - what could have been done better?
4. **Share examples** of when speaking up prevented a problem
5. **Personal commitment** - each person states one way they'll strengthen safety culture

**Reflection Questions:**
- How do I contribute to product safety every day?
- What would I want if my family's food came in packaging I made?
- How can I help new team members learn our safety culture?

## What Supervisors Look For

Your supervisor will observe:

- **Attitude** - genuine care for product safety, not just rule-following
- **Initiative** - taking action to prevent problems before they occur
- **Communication** - willingness to speak up about concerns or suggestions
- **Learning** - asking questions and seeking to understand the "why" behind procedures
- **Influence** - helping others understand and follow safety practices
- **Consistency** - maintaining standards even when under pressure

**Culture Development:** Your feedback and suggestions contribute to our annual culture plan review.
