**BRCGS Reference:** 6.2.1, 6.5.1-6.5.7

**Duration:** 45 minutes maximum

## REFRESHER TRAINING APPROACH

### **"LET'S VERIFY YOU CAN STILL DO THIS CORRECTLY"**

This refresher assumes you have previously completed Personal Hygiene Standards training. Today we will:
- **Verify** your continued competency in hygiene practices
- **Reinforce** critical habits that may drift over time  
- **Update** you on any procedure changes since your last training
- **Assess** your practical demonstration of hygiene standards

**Key Question:** Can you still demonstrate correct personal hygiene practices that protect our packaging products?

## REFRESHER LEARNING OBJECTIVES

By the end of this refresher, you will have **demonstrated** that you can still:

1. **Execute** proper hand washing technique without prompting or guidance
2. **Apply** correct hair coverage and protective clothing procedures
3. **Identify** personal hygiene violations and corrective actions needed
4. **Respond** appropriately to hygiene-related incidents or contamination risks

## HANDS-ON VERIFICATION ACTIVITIES

### **STATION 1: HAND WASHING TECHNIQUE**
**Competency Check:** Demonstrate 20-second hand washing without coaching

**Equipment Required:**
- Hand washing sink with soap dispensers
- Paper towels
- Timer/stopwatch
- Alcohol-based sanitiser

**Assessment Criteria:**
- [ ] Removes jewellery (except plain wedding ring)
- [ ] Uses warm water throughout
- [ ] Applies adequate soap amount
- [ ] Scrubs for full 20 seconds minimum
- [ ] Cleans between fingers and under nails
- [ ] Rinses thoroughly with water flowing away from hands
- [ ] Dries with single-use paper towel
- [ ] Applies sanitiser correctly

**Pass/Fail:** Must complete all 8 steps correctly without prompting

### **STATION 2: HAIR/BEARD COVERAGE VERIFICATION**
**Competency Check:** Properly don hair covering with no visible hair

**Equipment Required:**
- Hair nets (various sizes)
- Beard nets
- Hair clips/ties
- Mirror for self-verification

**Assessment Criteria:**
- [ ] All hair completely covered - no strands visible
- [ ] Hair net properly secured at all edges
- [ ] Beard/moustache covered if applicable
- [ ] Hair accessories removed or secured under covering
- [ ] No loose materials that could fall
- [ ] Comfortable fit allowing normal work movement

**Pass/Fail:** Zero tolerance for visible hair outside covering

### **STATION 3: UNIFORM COMPLIANCE CHECK**
**Competency Check:** Present yourself in complete, correct uniform

**Equipment Required:**
- Clean work clothing examples
- Safety shoes
- Protective aprons (where required)
- Lockers for personal items

**Assessment Criteria:**
- [ ] Clean, fresh uniform (no stains, odours, or damage)
- [ ] Closed-toe safety shoes in good repair
- [ ] Appropriate protective apron (wet areas/materials handling)
- [ ] Personal clothing stored in locker, not production area
- [ ] No loose clothing that could catch or contaminate
- [ ] Professional appearance suitable for packaging environment

**Pass/Fail:** Any visible contamination risk = fail

### **STATION 4: PERSONAL ITEMS INSPECTION**
**Competency Check:** Demonstrate compliance with personal items policy

**Equipment Required:**
- Locker access
- Metal detector wand (optional)
- Personal items storage containers

**Assessment Criteria:**
- [ ] Wedding ring only (plain band if worn)
- [ ] No watches, bracelets, or visible jewellery
- [ ] Mobile phone secured in locker
- [ ] No food, drinks, or personal medication in production areas
- [ ] Pockets empty of personal items
- [ ] No false nails, nail polish, or extensions

**Pass/Fail:** Any prohibited items in production area = fail

## PRACTICAL ASSESSMENT SCENARIOS

### **SCENARIO 1: Contamination Response**
**Situation:** You notice a colleague has a loose hair net with hair visible. What do you do?

**Correct Response:**
1. Immediately inform the colleague politely
2. Direct them to fix the issue before continuing work
3. Notify supervisor if colleague resists compliance
4. Check your own hair covering as reminder

### **SCENARIO 2: Hand Washing Emergency** 
**Situation:** The hand washing station runs out of soap during your shift. What are your options?

**Correct Response:**
1. Immediately report to supervisor - do not continue working
2. Use alternative hand washing station if available
3. Use alcohol-based sanitiser as temporary measure only
4. Do not handle product until proper hand washing is possible

### **SCENARIO 3: Personal Item Discovery**
**Situation:** You find a small metal screw in your uniform pocket during work. What should you do?

**Correct Response:**
1. Stop work immediately
2. Remove the screw carefully
3. Check if any product may have been contaminated
4. Report incident to supervisor with full details
5. Check all pockets before resuming work

## COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT

### **PASS CRITERIA**
- **Practical Stations:** Pass all 4 hands-on verification stations
- **Scenario Assessment:** Correctly respond to 2 out of 3 scenarios
- **Time Management:** Complete all activities within 45-minute timeframe
- **Professional Attitude:** Demonstrate understanding of hygiene importance

### **FAIL CRITERIA - IMMEDIATE RETRAINING REQUIRED**
- Failure to demonstrate any critical hygiene practice correctly
- Inability to identify hygiene violations when prompted
- Dismissive attitude towards hygiene requirements
- Repeated violations of personal items policy

### **REMEDIAL ACTION**
Personnel failing refresher assessment must:
1. **Immediate temporary restriction** from production areas
2. **Full initial hygiene training** within 48 hours
3. **Supervised practice** until competency demonstrated
4. **Manager notification** and documentation in personnel file
