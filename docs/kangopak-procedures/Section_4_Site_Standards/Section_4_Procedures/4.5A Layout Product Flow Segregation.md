# Procedure 4.5: Layout, Product Flow and Segregation

**Company:** Kangopak
**Document Type:** Operational Procedure
**Revision:** 1
**Revised Date:** 2025-09-24
**Document Owner:** Operations Manager
**Status:** Current

---

## Purpose

To establish and maintain site layout, product flow patterns, and segregation systems that minimize contamination risks, ensure product safety and quality, and comply with requirements for layout, product flow and segregation (Clause 4.5).

This procedure ensures that premises design, workflow patterns, and segregation systems protect product integrity throughout all stages of manufacturing, storage, and distribution.

## Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All areas of the Kangopak manufacturing facility
- Product flow from raw materials intake to finished goods dispatch
- Personnel movement patterns and access control
- Waste removal routes and segregation
- Storage area organization and segregation
- Manufacturing workflow design

## Responsibilities

### Operations Manager

- Overall responsibility for site layout compliance
- Approval of layout changes and modifications
- Coordination of cross-departmental layout reviews
- Ensure adequate space allocation for operations

### Quality Manager

- Assessment of contamination risks in layout design
- Validation of segregation effectiveness
- Review of product flow for quality compliance
- Approval of high-risk area designations

### Production Manager

- Implementation of manufacturing flow requirements

- Physical implementation of layout changes
- Maintenance of segregation barriers and markings
- Infrastructure support for product flow requirements
- Signage and marking systems maintenance

## Requirements Implementation

### 4.5.1 Current Site Map and Plan

**Official Requirement**: *"There shall be a current map, or plan of the site which defines: access points for personnel, travel routes for personnel/raw materials/work in progress/finished products, personnel facilities, routes for waste removal, flow of manufacturing operations, areas requiring additional control such as 'clean rooms', storage areas."*

#### Site Layout Map Requirements

The current site map shall clearly define and display:

**A. Personnel Access Points**

- Main entrance for employees
- Visitor entrance and control point
- Emergency exits and evacuation routes
- Restricted area access points
- Changing room and facility access points

**B. Material and Product Travel Routes**

- **Raw materials intake**: Designated receiving area and flow path
- **Work in progress flow**: Progression through manufacturing stages
- **Finished product flow**: Completion to packaging to storage routes
- **Cross-contamination prevention**: Separated paths where required
- **Loading bay access**: Dispatch and distribution routes

**C. Personnel Facilities**

- Office areas and administration zones
- Changing rooms and locker facilities
- Rest areas and canteen facilities
- Toilet facilities and washroom locations
- Training rooms and meeting areas

**D. Waste Removal Routes**

- General waste collection and removal paths
- Hazardous waste segregated collection routes
- Recycling material collection and storage
- Waste storage area locations
- External waste collection access points

**E. Manufacturing Operations Flow**

- Production line layouts and configurations
- Equipment positioning and workflow
- Quality control and inspection points
- Packaging operations areas
- Storage transitions during production

**F. Additional Control Areas**

- High-hygiene manufacturing zones
- Chemical storage areas (restricted access)
- Quality control laboratory areas
- Maintenance workshop areas
- Server room and electrical control areas

**G. Storage Areas**

- Raw materials storage (by category)
- Work in progress storage areas
- Finished goods storage (temperature zones)
- Cleaning chemical storage
- Maintenance supplies storage

#### Map Maintenance

- **Review Frequency**: Annual or upon any facility changes
- **Update Triggers**: Layout modifications, new equipment, process changes
- **Distribution**: Available at main entrance, management offices, and key control points
- **Digital Copies**: Maintained in document control system

### 4.5.2 Flow Arrangement for Contamination Minimization

**Official Requirement**: *"The flow from intake to dispatch shall be arranged to minimise the risk of contamination, or damage to the product."*

#### Product Flow Design Principles

**A. Linear Flow Pattern**

- **Raw Materials → Production → Packaging → Storage → Dispatch**
- Minimize cross-over points and back-tracking
- Clear directional flow with appropriate barriers
- Segregation of different product categories

**B. Contamination Prevention Design**

- **Physical separation** between raw materials and finished products
- **Elevated surfaces** for finished products to prevent floor contamination
- **Controlled access points** between different zones
- **Air flow management** to prevent contamination transfer
- **Dedicated equipment** for different process stages where required

**C. Product Protection Measures**

- **Covered transport** for materials susceptible to environmental contamination
- **Designated pathways** with appropriate flooring and drainage
- **Time-temperature sensitive routing** for products requiring special handling
- **Damage prevention** through appropriate handling surfaces and procedures

#### Flow Validation Requirements

- **Flow mapping**: Visual representation of all product movement
- **Risk assessment**: Contamination risk analysis at each transition point
- **Validation testing**: Periodic verification of contamination prevention effectiveness
- **Continuous monitoring**: Identification and correction of flow deviations

### 4.5.3 Sufficient Working Space and Storage Capacity

**Official Requirement**: *"Premises shall allow sufficient working space and storage capacity to enable all operations to be carried out properly under safe and hygienic conditions."*

#### Space Allocation Standards

**A. Manufacturing Areas**

- **Minimum clearances**: Adequate space around equipment for operation and cleaning
- **Personnel movement**: Sufficient space for safe personnel movement during operations
- **Material handling**: Space for safe material movement and positioning
- **Emergency access**: Clear access for emergency response and evacuation

**B. Storage Capacity Planning**

- **Raw materials capacity**: Adequate storage for production requirements plus safety stock
- **Work in progress storage**: Sufficient space for production flow management
- **Finished goods storage**: Capacity for production output and distribution planning
- **Segregation requirements**: Separate storage for different product categories

**C. Hygienic Design Requirements**

- **Easy cleaning access**: Space design allows effective cleaning and sanitization
- **Smooth surfaces**: Minimize ledges, gaps, and areas that collect contaminants
- **Drainage systems**: Appropriate drainage to prevent water accumulation
- **Ventilation space**: Adequate air circulation for hygiene maintenance

#### Capacity Management

- **Regular capacity reviews**: Assess adequacy of space allocation
- **Seasonal adjustments**: Plan for peak production and storage requirements
- **Expansion planning**: Identify needs for additional space or layout modifications
- **Efficiency optimization**: Regular review of space utilization effectiveness

### 4.5.4 Product Handling Area Standards

**Official Requirement**: *"Sorting, or other activities involving the direct handling of the product shall take place in areas that have, as a minimum, the same standards as manufacturing areas."*

#### Direct Product Handling Areas

Areas where product handling occurs shall maintain manufacturing area standards:

**A. Environmental Standards**

- **Temperature control**: Appropriate temperature maintenance for product safety
- **Humidity control**: Prevention of condensation and moisture-related contamination
- **Air quality management**: Filtration and air change rates equivalent to manufacturing areas
- **Lighting standards**: Adequate lighting for product inspection and safe handling

**B. Surface and Equipment Standards**

- **Food-grade surfaces**: All product contact surfaces suitable for food packaging materials
- **Easy cleaning design**: Surfaces designed for effective cleaning and sanitization
- **Equipment hygiene**: Regular cleaning and sanitization of handling equipment
- **Pest exclusion**: Effective pest control measures in all handling areas

**C. Personnel Standards**

- **Hygiene requirements**: Hand washing and sanitization facilities available
- **Protective clothing**: Appropriate protective clothing requirements
- **Training standards**: Personnel trained in product handling hygiene requirements
- **Health monitoring**: Health requirements equivalent to manufacturing areas

### 4.5.5 Contamination Risk Activity Segregation

**Official Requirement**: *"Activities that could produce a contamination risk, such as the removal of outer packing, shall be carried out in a designated, segregated area."*

#### High-Risk Activity Identification

Activities requiring segregation include:

**A. Packaging Removal Operations**

- **Outer packaging removal**: Removal of supplier packaging from raw materials
- **Damaged packaging handling**: Processing of damaged or compromised materials
- **Waste packaging processing**: Breakdown and disposal of used packaging materials
- **Returns processing**: Handling of returned products and materials

**B. Maintenance and Repair Activities**

- **Equipment maintenance**: Mechanical work that generates debris or contaminants
- **Infrastructure repairs**: Construction or repair work in production areas
- **Chemical applications**: Use of maintenance chemicals or lubricants
- **Tool and equipment cleaning**: Cleaning of maintenance tools and equipment

**C. Waste Processing Activities**

- **Waste sorting and processing**: Segregation of different waste categories
- **Cleaning chemical handling**: Preparation and use of cleaning solutions
- **Laboratory activities**: Testing and analysis that could generate contamination
- **Quality control rejection handling**: Processing of failed or rejected materials

#### Segregation Requirements

**A. Physical Segregation**

- **Designated areas**: Clearly marked and physically separated zones
- **Barrier systems**: Physical barriers to prevent contamination transfer
- **Access control**: Controlled entry and exit from segregated areas
- **Ventilation separation**: Independent air handling to prevent contamination spread

**B. Temporal Segregation**

- **Time-based separation**: High-risk activities conducted at specified times
- **Cleaning protocols**: Cleaning and sanitization between different activities
- **Personnel controls**: Personnel hygiene protocols when moving between areas
- **Equipment dedication**: Dedicated tools and equipment for segregated activities

### 4.5.6 Personnel Movement Design

**Official Requirement**: *"Sites shall be designed so that movement of all personnel is by simple, logical routes. If it is necessary to allow access through manufacturing areas, designated walkways shall be provided that ensure that product safety, legality and quality is not compromised."*

#### Personnel Route Design

**A. Simple, Logical Routes**

- **Clear pathways**: Well-marked routes that are intuitive and easy to follow
- **Directional flow**: Routes that support efficient movement without confusion
- **Minimal decision points**: Route design that minimizes navigation complexity
- **Visual guidance**: Signage and markings to support route following

**B. Manufacturing Area Access Control**

- **Designated walkways**: Clearly marked walkways through production areas
- **Physical separation**: Walkways physically separated from production operations
- **Height differentiation**: Elevated walkways or designated floor areas
- **Contamination barriers**: Systems to prevent contamination from foot traffic

**C. Access Authorization Systems**

- **Personnel authorization**: Control of who may access different areas
- **Visitor control**: Managed access for visitors and contractors
- **Training requirements**: Personnel training on route usage and restrictions
- **Emergency access**: Emergency personnel access routes and procedures

#### Route Validation and Maintenance

**A. Route Effectiveness Assessment**

- **Flow analysis**: Regular assessment of personnel movement patterns
- **Contamination risk evaluation**: Assessment of contamination risks from personnel movement
- **Efficiency optimization**: Identification of route improvements
- **Safety validation**: Confirmation of route safety for all users

**B. Maintenance Requirements**

- **Pathway maintenance**: Regular cleaning and maintenance of walkways
- **Signage maintenance**: Maintenance and updating of route signage
- **Barrier maintenance**: Maintenance of physical separation barriers
- **Lighting maintenance**: Adequate lighting for safe route navigation
