# Procedure 4.5: Emergency Procedures and Response

**Company:** Kangopak
**Document Type:** Operational Procedure
**Revision:** 1
**Revised Date:** 2025-09-24
**Document Owner:** Operations Manager
**Status:** Current

---

## Purpose

To establish comprehensive emergency response procedures that protect personnel safety, minimize property damage, ensure business continuity, and maintain compliance with requirements for site security and safety standards.

This procedure ensures that Kangopak has appropriate emergency procedures to protect personnel and maintain security during crisis situations, supporting requirements under clauses 4.4 (Security) and site standards requirements.

## Scope

This procedure applies to all Kangopak personnel, contractors, and visitors on site, covering all types of emergencies that may occur during normal operations, including:

- Fire emergencies
- Medical emergencies
- Security incidents
- Natural disasters
- Chemical spills or exposure
- Power failures
- Equipment failures with safety implications

## Responsibilities

### Operations Manager

- Overall responsibility for emergency preparedness
- Ensure emergency procedures are current and effective
- Coordinate emergency response training programs
- Interface with external emergency services

### Emergency Response Team

- **Fire Wardens**: Coordinate fire evacuations and response
- **First Aid Officers**: Provide medical assistance
- **Security Officers**: Manage security-related emergencies
- **Floor Wardens**: Assist with evacuations and personnel accountability

### All Personnel

- Know and follow emergency procedures
- Participate in emergency drills and training
- Report emergencies immediately using established protocols
- Assist in emergency response as trained and directed

## Emergency Response Procedures

### 4.5.1 Fire Emergencies

#### Small Fires (Controllable)

1. **Assess the situation** - Determine if fire can be safely controlled
2. **Alert others** - Shout "FIRE" to alert nearby personnel
3. **Attempt control** - Use appropriate fire extinguisher if trained
4. **Call for help** - Activate fire alarm if fire spreads
5. **Evacuate if unsuccessful** - Follow evacuation procedures

#### Large Fires (Evacuation Required)

1. **Activate fire alarm** - Use nearest fire alarm call point
2. **Call emergency services** - Dial 10177 (Fire Department)
3. **Evacuate immediately** - Follow designated evacuation routes
4. **Report to assembly point** - Designated parking area
5. **Account for personnel** - Fire wardens check attendance
6. **Assist emergency services** - Provide site information and access

### 4.5.2 Medical Emergencies

#### Minor Injuries

1. **Assess injury severity** - Determine if first aid is sufficient
2. **Provide first aid** - Use trained first aid officers
3. **Record incident** - Complete incident report form
4. **Monitor condition** - Watch for deterioration
5. **Seek medical advice** if condition worsens

#### Serious Injuries/Medical Emergencies

1. **Call emergency services** - Dial 10177 (Ambulance)
2. **Provide immediate care** - First aid while awaiting ambulance
3. **Clear access routes** - Ensure ambulance can reach patient
4. **Notify management** - Inform Operations Manager immediately
5. **Preserve scene** - If injury-related, maintain for investigation
6. **Complete reporting** - Full incident investigation and reporting

### 4.5.3 Security Emergencies

#### Unauthorized Access/Intruders

1. **Do not confront** - Maintain personal safety priority
2. **Alert security** - Contact security officer immediately
3. **Call police** - Dial 10111 if threat is serious
4. **Secure area** - Isolate affected area if possible
5. **Account for personnel** - Ensure all personnel are safe
6. **Document incident** - Complete security incident report

#### Theft/Vandalism

1. **Secure scene** - Preserve evidence
2. **Contact police** - File report if required
3. **Notify management** - Inform Operations Manager
4. **Document losses** - Complete inventory of damages/missing items
5. **Review security** - Assess need for additional measures

### 4.5.4 Chemical Spills and Exposure

#### Small Spills (Controllable)

1. **Alert others** - Warn personnel in area
2. **Stop source** if safely possible
3. **Contain spill** - Use spill kits and absorbents
4. **Clean up** - Following chemical safety procedures
5. **Dispose properly** - Use designated waste procedures
6. **Report incident** - Complete spill report form

#### Large Spills or Exposure

1. **Evacuate area** - Clear affected zone immediately
2. **Call emergency services** - Hazmat response if required
3. **Provide first aid** - For any exposed personnel
4. **Isolate area** - Prevent further exposure
5. **Notify authorities** - Environmental compliance requirements
6. **Document thoroughly** - Full incident investigation

### 4.5.5 Power Failures

#### Planned Outages

1. **Prepare systems** - Shut down equipment safely
2. **Secure products** - Protect work in progress
3. **Maintain communications** - Use backup communication systems
4. **Monitor conditions** - Temperature-sensitive areas
5. **Resume operations** - Systematic restart procedures

#### Unplanned Outages

1. **Ensure safety** - Emergency lighting and safe evacuation routes
2. **Assess duration** - Contact utility provider
3. **Protect products** - Immediate action for perishables
4. **Activate backup** - Generator systems if available
5. **Communication** - Keep all personnel informed
6. **Document impact** - Product safety and quality implications

## Evacuation Procedures

### Evacuation Routes

- **Primary Route**: Main entrance via production floor
- **Secondary Route**: Loading bay exit via warehouse
- **Emergency Route**: Fire exit near offices

### Assembly Point

**Location**: Designated parking area (northeast corner of property)
**Features**:

- Safe distance from buildings
- Clear vehicle access for emergency services
- Weather shelter available
- Emergency contact information posted

### Evacuation Process

1. **Alarm activation** - Fire alarm or PA announcement
2. **Immediate response** - Stop work, secure area if safe to do so
3. **Exit calmly** - Use nearest designated evacuation route
4. **Assist others** - Help disabled or injured personnel
5. **Report to assembly point** - Do not leave site
6. **Attendance check** - Fire wardens account for all personnel
7. **Await instructions** - Do not re-enter until authorized
