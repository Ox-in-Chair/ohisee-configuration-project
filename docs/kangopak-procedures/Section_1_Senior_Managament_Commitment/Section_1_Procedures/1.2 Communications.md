# Procedure 1.2: BRC Communications Procedure

**Company:** Kangopak
**Document Type:** Operational Procedure
**Revision:** 4
**Revised Date:** 2025-09-02
**Document Owner:** Director
**Status:** Current

---

## Purpose

To ensure that policies, practices and information are consistently and effectively communicated throughout our organisation, to visitors, contractors and interested parties and that employees and contractors are informed on Product Safety, Quality and legal matters and they have the opportunity to participate and make suggestions in this regard.

## Method

### Customer Complaints

Customer Complaints are handled as per the Complaint handling procedure

### Customer Enquiries

Product information including essential customer safety handling, instructions, storage, application and order handling are done as per the Customer focus and contract review procedure

### Suppliers, Contractors and Visitors

Supplier communication with regard to Supplier Agreement, Service Level Agreements, specification establishment, audits and Risk Rating is handled as per the Supplier approval and performance monitoring procedure

Communication with visitors and contractors are managed as per Personal Hygiene procedure and Visitors Register.

### Emergency Situations

In the event of an emergency situation the Director is immediately contacted and informed.

Based on the severity of the incident, the appropriate government/media agencies are informed after consultation with the internal legal department/representatives.

## Internal Communication

### General

Aspects relating to Product Safety and Quality are communicated to all relevant personnel in the organisation. Product Safety and Quality Information are distributed, collected and communicated (but not limited) by the following tools:

- Employee Induction Program
- Notice boards
- Electronic mail
- Posters and Signage
- Meetings:
  - Production meetings (1.4F2)/WhatsApp Group
  - Staff Meetings (1.4F5) /WhatsApp Group
  - Management Meetings (PS&QMS 1.4F4) Management/Commercial WhatsApp Group
  - Annual Management Review refer to Management review minutes (1.4F1)
  - Risk Meeting (1.4F3)

The effectiveness of internal communication processes are evaluated on an ongoing basis through training programs, audits, inspections and informal discussions.

### HACCP Study Review Communications

To ensure that the product safety and quality management system is not compromised, changes to the following elements trigger a HACCP study review and communicated to all relevant personnel. Refer to HACCP procedure:

- Raw Materials
- Standards and Specification
- Documentation and Records
- Production Systems and Equipment
- Production Premises, Location of Equipment, Surrounding Environment
- Cleaning, Sanitation programmes and Chemicals
- Distribution Service
- Suppliers
- Statutory and Regulatory Requirements
- Contractor Contract Amendment
- Personnel qualifications levels and /or allocation of responsibilities and authorisations
- Knowledge regarding Product Safety Hazards and Control Measures
- Any other conditions that have an impact on Product Safety

### Computer Based Systems

**Pastel** – finance management system. This system is access controlled and only limited to designed staff as per management request.

The Product Safety and Quality is maintained on the shared drive giving read access to relevant personnel. Refer to the Document control procedure

**Email** – Is a computer based tool to facilitate internal and external communication and is password protected.

### Notice Boards

Notice boards are used in the production area to communicate product safety and Quality related information.

## Performance Measures and Reporting

Number and nature of:

- Communication related audit findings
- Customer Complaints

These Performance Measures are reported to top management through Management Review.
