# Procedure 3.8: BRC Record Keeping Procedure

**Company:** Kangopak
**Document Type:** Operational Procedure
**Revision:** 4
**Revised Date:** 2025-09-02
**Document Owner:** Systems Co-Ordinator
**Status:** Current

---

## Objective

To ensure that records are safely retained for an agreed period and that these records are traceable to the specific activity or item to which they pertain.

To ensure that records are maintained to demonstrate the effective control of product safety, legality and quality.

## Method

### Table of Records

A table of records is developed within each procedural document. This table defines and lists the records maintained and the controls applied.

### Form Template Authorisation and Amendment

Form template authorisation and amendment is done as per the Control of Document procedure.

### Form Template Issue

All form templates have a unique number for easy identification. The issuing of a new or amended form template is conducted as per the Control of Documents Procedure.

### Revision Control

Only the latest version of each form template is available on the shared drive. The previous version is retained on file at the discretion of the Operations Manager.

### Records Input Responsibility

The job title responsible for data entry is defined for each record in the records table. It is the responsibility of each individual working with forms/records to ensure that content are legible. Changes to records are initialled and dated. Any alterations to records are authorised and justification recorded.

### Records Collation, Filing and Indexing

The Systems Co-Ordinator is responsible for ensuring records are appropriately collated, filed and indexed within 48 hours of completion to ensure records are readily retrievable when required.

### Records Retention Location and Period

The period of retention for records relate to the usable life of the packaging and product it is designed to contain and respect any customer requirements.

The retention location and period of retention of these records are defined in the records table.

### Discarding of Records

The authority for discarding records is defined in the table.

### Review of Records

Records are reviewed in accordance with the requirements of the relevant Procedure.

### Record Retrieval

Records are readily retrievable by virtue of collation, filing and indexing in a manner appropriate to the Records in question. Access to Records is controlled by virtue of Password access (Electronic) or Site Security (Paper).

### Records Protection

Physical access to records is controlled by means of the access security system on site. Refer to Security procedure.

Electronic access to records is controlled by means of password protection on the company shared drive.

Electronic records are subjected to back up recovery as defined in the Security procedure.

## Records

- Table of Records
- Form Templates
- Record Retention Schedule
- Access Control Logs
- Backup Recovery Records
