# Procedure 5.6A: Product Inspection, Testing and Measuring

**Company:** Kangopak
**Document Type:** Operational Procedure
**Revision:** 1
**Revised Date:** 2025-09-24
**Document Owner:** Quality Manager
**Status:** Current

---

## Purpose

To establish comprehensive product inspection, testing, and measuring procedures that ensure product safety, legality, and quality throughout manufacturing operations, in full compliance with requirements for product inspection, testing and measuring (Clause 5.6).

This procedure ensures that appropriate testing and inspection systems are in place to verify product conformance to specifications and regulatory requirements at all critical stages of production.

## Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All product inspection, testing, and measuring activities
- Raw materials, work-in-progress, and finished products
- In-line inspection and testing equipment
- Laboratory testing procedures and external testing arrangements
- Sample management and handling procedures
- Personnel competency requirements for testing activities

## Responsibilities

### Quality Manager

- Overall responsibility for testing and inspection systems
- Approval of testing procedures and specifications
- Coordination with external testing laboratories
- Review and approval of testing results and corrective actions

### Laboratory Supervisor

- Implementation of testing procedures and protocols
- Management of testing equipment and calibration
- Training and competency assessment of testing personnel
- Quality control of testing results and documentation

### Production Manager

- Implementation of in-line inspection procedures
- Coordination of production testing schedules
- Personnel training on inspection requirements
- Integration of testing with production workflows

### Quality Control Technicians

- Execution of testing and inspection procedures
- Sample collection and preparation
- Equipment operation and maintenance
- Results recording and reporting

## Requirements Implementation

### 5.6.1 Determination of Testing and Inspection Needs

**Official Requirement**: *"The site shall determine the need for product testing, inspection or measuring equipment to ensure product safety, legality and quality."*

#### Testing Needs Assessment Process

**A. Risk-Based Assessment**

- **Product safety risks**: Identification of safety-critical parameters requiring testing
- **Legal compliance requirements**: Regulatory testing requirements for packaging materials
- **Quality assurance needs**: Quality parameters essential for customer satisfaction
- **Process validation requirements**: Testing needed to validate manufacturing processes

**B. Testing Parameter Identification**
Critical testing parameters include:

**Physical Properties Testing**:

- **Dimensional accuracy**: Length, width, thickness measurements
- **Strength properties**: Tensile strength, puncture resistance, seal strength
- **Barrier properties**: Moisture barrier, oxygen barrier, grease resistance
- **Surface properties**: Smoothness, adhesion, coating integrity

**Safety and Compliance Testing**:

- **Migration testing**: Potential migration of substances to food contact
- **Heavy metals analysis**: Lead, cadmium, mercury, chromium levels
- **Microbiological testing**: Bacterial contamination levels
- **Chemical residue testing**: Solvent residues, printing ink components

**Quality Parameters**:

- **Visual appearance**: Color consistency, print quality, surface defects
- **Functional performance**: Opening characteristics, dispensing functionality
- **Aging and stability**: Long-term performance under storage conditions

#### Testing Equipment Requirements

**A. In-Line Testing Equipment**

- **Dimensional measurement systems**: Automated measurement of critical dimensions
- **Vision inspection systems**: Automated detection of visual defects
- **Seal integrity testing**: Testing of heat seals and closures
- **Weight verification systems**: Product weight verification and control

**B. Laboratory Testing Equipment**

- **Physical testing equipment**: Tensile testing, puncture testing, barrier testing
- **Chemical analysis equipment**: Spectrophotometers, chromatography, titrators
- **Microbiological testing equipment**: Incubators, microscopes, culture media
- **Environmental testing equipment**: Temperature/humidity chambers, UV exposure

### 5.6.2 Validation of Testing Procedures

**Official Requirement**: *"The inspection or testing procedures used by the site shall be validated to ensure their accuracy and reproducibility. Recognised test procedures shall be used, where available."*

#### Testing Procedure Validation Requirements

**A. Recognized Standard Methods**
Where available, testing shall follow recognized standards:

**International Standards**:

- **ISO standards**: ISO 2556 (tensile testing), ISO 11607 (packaging systems)
- **ASTM standards**: ASTM D882 (tensile properties), ASTM F88 (seal strength)
- **EN standards**: European packaging material testing standards
- **FDA methods**: FDA-approved migration testing methods

**B. Method Validation Parameters**
For all testing methods, validation shall demonstrate:

**Accuracy**:

- **Reference material testing**: Use of certified reference materials
- **Recovery studies**: Testing of known samples to verify accuracy
- **Comparative testing**: Comparison with recognized methods or laboratories

**Reproducibility**:

- **Repeatability**: Same operator, same equipment, same conditions
- **Intermediate precision**: Different operators, different days
- **Reproducibility**: Different equipment, different locations

**Specificity**:

- **Interference testing**: Verification that method measures intended parameter
- **Matrix effects**: Assessment of sample matrix impact on results
- **Selectivity**: Ability to distinguish target parameter from interferences

#### Validation Documentation

- **Validation protocols**: Detailed procedure for method validation
- **Validation reports**: Results of validation studies
- **Method procedures**: Validated procedures for routine use
- **Ongoing validation**: Periodic revalidation requirements

### 5.6.3 Product Inspection and Testing Procedures

**Official Requirement**: *"Product inspection and testing procedures shall be in place, and carried out at appropriate stages in manufacturing to demonstrate that the finished product is within the tolerances laid down in the agreed product specification."*

#### Testing Frequency and Sample Quantity

**A. Risk-Based Testing Frequency**
Testing frequency determined by:

- **Product safety criticality**: Higher frequency for safety-critical parameters
- **Process stability**: More frequent testing for less stable processes
- **Customer requirements**: Customer-specified testing frequencies
- **Regulatory requirements**: Legal minimum testing frequencies
- **Historical performance**: Frequency based on past performance data

**B. Sample Quantity Requirements**

- **Statistical validity**: Sample sizes based on statistical requirements
- **Representative sampling**: Samples representative of production lot
- **Customer specifications**: Sample sizes per customer requirements
- **Standard method requirements**: Sample sizes per test method requirements

**C. Testing Stages**

**Raw Materials Testing**:

- **Incoming inspection**: Visual inspection and key parameter testing
- **Certificate of analysis review**: Verification of supplier test results
- **Periodic verification testing**: Confirmation of supplier testing accuracy

**In-Process Testing**:

- **Setup verification**: Testing at production setup to confirm parameters
- **Ongoing monitoring**: Regular testing during production runs
- **Change verification**: Testing after any process adjustments

**Finished Product Testing**:

- **End-of-run testing**: Final verification before release
- **Release testing**: Testing required for product release
- **Periodic monitoring**: Ongoing testing of released products

#### Authorized Personnel Requirements

**A. Personnel Authorization**

- **Competency assessment**: Verification of technical competency
- **Training completion**: Completion of relevant training programs
- **Authorization documentation**: Formal authorization to conduct testing
- **Ongoing competency**: Regular assessment of continued competency

**B. Training Requirements**

- **Technical training**: Training on testing procedures and equipment
- **Safety training**: Safety requirements for testing activities
- **Quality system training**: Understanding of quality system requirements
- **Record keeping training**: Training on documentation requirements

#### Results Recording and Review

**A. Results Documentation**

- **Complete results recording**: All test results documented
- **Traceability**: Clear traceability to samples and production lots
- **Anomaly documentation**: Documentation of any unusual results
- **Corrective action records**: Documentation of any corrective actions taken

**B. Results Review Process**

- **Technical review**: Review by qualified personnel for technical accuracy
- **Trend analysis**: Analysis of results for trends and patterns
- **Specification compliance**: Verification of compliance with specifications
- **Customer communication**: Communication of results to customers when required

#### Sample Management

**A. Sample Handling**

- **Sample identification**: Clear identification and labeling
- **Chain of custody**: Documented chain of custody for samples
- **Storage conditions**: Appropriate storage conditions to maintain integrity
- **Sample security**: Security measures to prevent tampering

**B. Sample Disposition**
Sample disposition options include:

- **Retention**: Retention for specified period for reference
- **Return to stock**: Return of unused samples to production
- **Reworking/recycling**: Processing of samples for reuse
- **Disposal**: Appropriate disposal of used or expired samples

### 5.6.4 Integrated Testing Equipment Procedures

**Official Requirement**: *"Where inspection or testing equipment is integrated into the manufacturing operations and is critical to product safety, legality and quality, the site shall establish and implement procedures for the operation and testing of the equipment."*

#### Equipment Operation Procedures

**A. Setup Procedures**

- **Equipment calibration**: Verification of equipment calibration before use
- **Parameter setting**: Proper setting of equipment parameters
- **Reference standard verification**: Use of reference standards for setup verification
- **Operator verification**: Confirmation of operator competency

**B. Alert and Rejection Systems**
Equipment shall be capable of:

- **Alerting operators**: Visual and/or audible alerts for out-of-specification conditions
- **Automatic rejection**: Automatic rejection or identification of non-conforming products
- **Process shutdown**: Automatic process shutdown for critical failures
- **Documentation**: Automatic documentation of alerts and rejections

#### Equipment Testing and Verification

**A. Verification Frequency**
Equipment verification required at:

**Start and End of Manufacturing Run**:

- **Setup verification**: Confirmation of proper equipment setup
- **Performance verification**: Verification of equipment performance throughout run
- **End-of-run verification**: Confirmation of continued performance at run completion

**Risk-Based Frequency**:

- **High-risk processes**: More frequent verification for critical processes
- **Process capability**: Frequency based on process control capability
- **Failure impact assessment**: Frequency based on potential impact of equipment failure

**Manufacturer Recommendations**:

- **Preventive maintenance schedules**: Following equipment manufacturer schedules
- **Calibration intervals**: Manufacturer-recommended calibration frequencies
- **Performance verification**: Manufacturer-specified performance checks

### 5.6.5 Current Procedures and Reference Samples

**Official Requirement**: *"Inspection and testing procedures and customer-approved reference samples (where required) shall be of the most recent version and be available at the location where the activity is conducted."*

#### Document Control for Testing Procedures

**A. Current Version Control**

- **Version identification**: Clear identification of current procedure versions
- **Distribution control**: Controlled distribution of current procedures
- **Obsolete version removal**: Removal of obsolete procedure versions
- **Change notification**: Notification of procedure changes to relevant personnel

**B. Location Requirements**

- **Point-of-use availability**: Procedures available where testing is conducted
- **Digital access**: Electronic access to current procedures
- **Physical copies**: Physical copies where digital access is not practical
- **Emergency procedures**: Access to procedures during system failures

#### Reference Sample Management

**A. Customer-Approved Reference Samples**

- **Approval documentation**: Documentation of customer approval for reference samples
- **Sample identification**: Clear identification and traceability
- **Storage conditions**: Appropriate storage to maintain sample integrity
- **Expiry management**: Management of reference sample expiry and replacement

**B. Reference Sample Use**

- **Comparison procedures**: Procedures for comparing products to reference samples
- **Visual assessment**: Visual comparison procedures and acceptance criteria
- **Physical testing**: Use of reference samples for physical testing verification
- **Documentation**: Documentation of reference sample use and results

### 5.6.6 Testing Facility Requirements

**Official Requirement**: *"Where testing critical to product safety or legality is undertaken, the internal or external testing facility shall have gained recognised accreditation or operate in accordance with the requirements and principles of ISO/IEC17025, including competency testing where applicable."*

#### Accreditation Requirements

**A. Testing Criticality Assessment**
Determination of which testing is critical to product safety or legality:

- **Safety-critical parameters**: Parameters directly affecting product safety
- **Legal compliance parameters**: Parameters required for regulatory compliance
- **Risk assessment**: Assessment of risk associated with different parameters

**B. Accreditation Requirements for Critical Testing**

**Internal Laboratory Requirements**:

- **ISO/IEC 17025 compliance**: Operation according to ISO/IEC 17025 principles
- **Competency testing**: Participation in relevant proficiency testing programs
- **Quality management system**: ISO/IEC 17025-compliant quality management
- **Personnel competency**: Demonstrated competency of testing personnel

**External Laboratory Requirements**:

- **Accreditation verification**: Verification of accreditation for relevant testing
- **Scope verification**: Verification that accreditation covers required testing
- **Competency assessment**: Assessment of laboratory competency
- **Regular monitoring**: Ongoing monitoring of external laboratory performance

#### Method Validation for Non-Accredited Methods

**A. Justification Requirements**
Where accredited or reference methods are not used:

- **Technical justification**: Technical justification for method selection
- **Validation evidence**: Evidence of method validation
- **Comparison studies**: Comparison with recognized methods where possible
- **Risk assessment**: Assessment of risk associated with non-accredited methods
